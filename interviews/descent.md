## What this map is for

The May 10 architecture organized the book as two movements — Diagnosis and
Initiation — with six initiatory chapters threaded through the existing
twenty-four diagnostic chapters. That architecture worked as a transitional
structure. It is no longer true.

The book is one movement: a descent that does not end. The diagnosis *is* the
descent. The texts arrive as lanterns in the order the descent makes them
legible. The reader walks the descent the wizard already walked, with the
wizard's notes, until the reader can keep walking without the notes. There is
no Station Last. The descent continues for the rest of the reader's life,
lived inside ordinary days that no longer mean what they used to mean.

This map names the stations of that descent. Each station is the reader's
interior condition at one point on the path. Each station is served by
existing chapter material, reframed — and in some cases by material not yet
written, drawn from biographical fragments still in Jason's inbox or still
unspoken. The map identifies what serves what, what's missing, and what's
blocked on which inbox file.

## The reader, plainly

The reader is a leftist on the path to radicalization, anywhere along that
path. The book must be legible to someone joining at any station — but the
deepest reading comes from walking the descent from the beginning.

The reader has a private moment. They've told people about it. The people
received it as a data point. The reader has always half-known it was a
glitch — a moment when the world they were living in showed itself to be a
particular world, assembled by particular hands, not the world. They have
carried this without language for it. The book trusts this carrying. The
book's first move is not to inform the reader of structural injustice. It is
to make legible to the reader that *the moment was the moment*. The wedge is
already in their hand. The book is teaching them how to lean on it.

## The four register-keepers

The book's voice is held by four figures, none alone sufficient, all four
needed in proportion:

**Coyote** — Truth in plain sight, read as joke. The book's title is Coyote.
The dismissal is the seal. The reader who laughs and reads anyway is the
reader the book is for. *Coyote is gravity that knows when to wink.*

**Nasruddin** — The lamp under the streetlight. The fool whose mistake is
sharper than the room's correctness. *Find the key where the light is — but
notice that the light is where someone hung the lamp.* The book uses
Nasruddin energy to put things at oblique angles, so the reader sees them
freshly without the cliché armor going up.

**Kropotkin** — The prince who walked away to count glaciers and discovered
the dominant theory was wrong about what nature rewards. *Field-notes from
the outside.* The book stays Kropotkin-grounded: when it argues, it argues
from the evidence; when it tells you what something is, it tells you who
checked. The fool with field notes.

**Castaneda** — The apprentice. *Seeing is a learnable practice.* The
previous world must die for the new world to be perceptible. The reader is
the apprentice. The book is teaching by showing the apprentice how to lose
what they thought they knew without losing themselves in the losing.
Whether Don Juan existed literally is beside the point; the form is the
form, and the form is what the wizard transmits.

Together: the holy fool with field notes who jokes because the joke is the
lantern. None of these alone. All four threaded.

## The cover-story logic

The book performs, on the reader, what the book describes. Production of
subjectivity arrives in the manuscript as the phrase that grabbed Jason in
May 2020 and took five years to become legible. The texts in this book will
do the same to the reader. The reader will encounter a phrase, a scene, a
sentence that grabs them before they have the question. They will carry it
for years. Then they will arrive at the question, and the phrase will
already be theirs.

The wizard does not announce themselves. The title does the announcing,
loudly enough that it reads as joke. The seal is the dismissal. The book
itself proceeds as if the announcement had been heard and accepted —
because for the reader who's still here, it has been.

Do not break the cover. Do not, anywhere in the text, *explain* the
wizard's-handbook frame. The form must demonstrate what the form cannot
say. The reader who needs the explanation has not done the descent. The
reader who has, doesn't need it.

## What is not in this map

This map is the **descent architecture**, not the chapter list. The
relationship between stations and chapters is many-to-many: some existing
chapters serve a single station; some serve fragments of two or three; some
stations need material from chapters that don't exist yet (the four
undrafted initiation chapters, in particular, are not freestanding chapters
anymore — they are station material distributed where the descent needs
them).

After this map is approved, the next pass is **Phase 1: Material
Assignment** — taking the 26 existing chapter files and assigning each
section to a station, marking what's reusable, what needs reframing, what
needs to be cut, and what's missing.

## The Stations

Six capacities. Six stations. Plus a Station Zero, which is where the
reader enters before any capacity has been built.

Each station below has:

- **The reader at entry** — interior condition arriving here.
- **What arrives** — the lantern. Usually a text. Sometimes a scene. Sometimes both.
- **Biographical anchor** — Jason's parallel material that makes the station concrete.
- **What the reader is capable of at exit** — the capacity built.
- **Existing material that serves** — chapters or fragments to reuse.
- **What's missing** — material yet to be written, by whom (Claude/Jason), and what it needs.
- **Inbox dependencies** — unanswered dyad files this station is blocked on.
- **Coyote / Nasruddin / Kropotkin / Castaneda** — which register dominates here, and what it does.
- **Risks** — known traps; what could go wrong in the writing.
