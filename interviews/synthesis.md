## What we now know

### The formation (before the book's timeline)

**The martial arts tradition** — mentioned once, in passing, as "my tradition" and "the discipline I was given." Not yet named. Not yet in the book. Something formative happened here that gave Jason an embodied practice of discipline and honor that he could not and would not transfer inappropriately. This is prior to the tech years, possibly prior to the South. Needs a question.

**The South** — named as one of three things Jason was running from: tech dissociation, dehumanizing upbringing, personal disappointment at 30. The shrug in Richmond is in the book. The formation of the shrug in Jason's own nervous system is not yet in the book. Where does it live now?

**The tech years (10 years)** — "dissociation" is the word used. Something was built. Something may still be running. Jason's relationship to the Stallman/Berners-Lee/Torvalds lineage is uncharted.

### The descent sequence

**Burnout #1 (software):** Got slower and slower. Stumbled. Couldn't get back up. Lay there for 4 months. The only thing that got him up: going to the woods, in community. Applied to the wilderness program while on the floor. Was accepted. Went.

**The wilderness years:** Counter-hegemony without the word. The deer chapter. The children who became older than themselves. "Easy mode" — the guide frame creates the aura. Wonder fills a container you've built. The lesson that will take the restaurant to complete: connection before everything else.

**The Naropa decision (in Bali):** Accepted to Resilient Leadership at Naropa. Would have studied with Joanna Macy. Cost: ~$250K. Said no. Said no while surrounded by people living beautifully in poverty, holding an acceptance letter that felt like it was missing something essential about what he was looking at. The restaurant was what he did instead. *The thing Naropa would have given him: connection comes first. Every time. Not knowledge. Not discipline. Not correctness. Not excellence. Connection.*

**Burnout #2 (restaurant):** Different from #1. He saw collapse coming and got out before it arrived. The sign, what collapse looked like from close enough to see it — not yet answered.

### The restaurant as curriculum

**Production of subjectivity** — the phrase arrived from *Commonwealth*, May 2020, two months after the world ended. Haunted for five years. Had the answer, needed the question. The question only fully arrived after the restaurant closed. Five years of gestation.

**The kid** — the central case study of the restaurant's resistance. Intelligent, motivated, talented (self-taught artist). Responded only to authoritarian modes of perception. Without explicit permission, instruction, external discipline: unable to activate. Jason's tools assumed a self the system had not allowed the kid to build. The connection was missing before the scaffold went up — and Jason didn't see it until he was attacking the wrong problem.

*"As did we all"* — the situation was intolerable for everyone, not just the kid. The commons attempt exhausting for those holding it.

**The crossing from seeker to guide** — happened at the restaurant. Heard his words coming out of customers' and employees' mouths. Not because they were instructed to — because it made sense to them. Realized he had to step up his game because they were catching on.

**The confidence after closing** — explained production of subjectivity after the restaurant closed with the confidence of someone who had studied it for years. Because he had. The restaurant was the curriculum. The closure was the graduation.

### The perceptual shift

**The crow cloud and the barred owl** — Ravenna Park, Seattle. Not listening and analyzing — *feeling what they were feeling*. A cloud of crows around a barred owl. First owl in daylight.

**The robin a few months later** — sounds nothing like a crow. But the feeling was the same. Looked where the robin was looking. The owl, staring back.

**The principle** — if there's an owl nearby, the birds will tell you. The way that can be named is not the way. Once you've seen a creature, you can see them. Something opens that cannot be articulated as "how to see a muskrat."

**The commons of perception** — a "we" practice. Erin is part of this. What she sees that Jason doesn't, and vice versa — not yet answered.

### What the book is doing

**Psychic architecture** — Jason's phrase for what he's doing. Sculpting a way of being for others to inhabit. Ethically fraught. Doing his best. The line between architecture and manipulation — not yet answered.

**The reader** — privileged liberals open to radicalization. A specific person in mind, not yet named. What they're doing right now that the book is trying to interrupt — not yet answered.

**The gates** — the 2013 essay's ending: *become again a part of the world; come home to hear and tell the oldest stories of all.* Never said out loud outside ceremonial context. "That's the other realm bleeding into this one. I intend to throw those gates wide open."

## Update: Re-Enchanting the World answers (2026-05-10)

**The progressive layering** — not a single shift but an accumulation. Thought they were making up silly little voices. Realized over time they were actually learning. Birds confirmed it by getting closer. *As we tried to understand them, they understood us.* The reciprocity is the argument.

**Erin's complementary perception** — she notices space, he notices systems. Through her space he reaches the habitus and socius. Through his systems she reaches hidden spaces and spaces too large to see without the mind's eye (like a city). The commons of perception produces something neither could access alone. Filed as central to Re-Enchanting chapter and Simondon's pre-individual field argument.

**Eldritch in the old sense** — not Lovecraftian horror but *a new nature*. Hybrid, otherworldly. Information technology and psychedelics fit. "That's why we need the wisdom of the ancient shamans — and anyone else that can stand the Far Outside." This is the chapter's spine: the Far Outside requires someone who can stand it. The shamanic lineage as the people who maintained the practice through the centuries of burning.

**New thread**: "What we're doing here would fit the bill" — the book itself, this collaboration, is an eldritch thing. Filed as a possible Centaur chapter note.
