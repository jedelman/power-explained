# Wizard Lexicon — the book's core concepts in wizard register

The consistent vocabulary for *How to Become a Wizard and Save the World*.
Every chapter holds the same register for the same idea — this is voice
consistency at the level of concept. Built collaboratively with Jason during
the sequencing pass (2026-05-26).

## The engine

- **The field:** enclosure ⇄ commons. The one axis everything moves along.
- **The casting:** incantation lays subjectivity along the axis; what it casts
  is enchantment — or, run backward, disenchantment.
- **The irreducible:** magic is the transformative capacity of singularities
  acting on each other — your gift, the land's secret ways. The averaging
  machine cannot capture it; it is what perforates the enclosure.
- **The apparatus:** the grimoire (the tool), the wizard (who wields it),
  initiation (the becoming).

## The master principle

Every concept here is a tool that was turned against us and can be taken back.
The spell becomes the counter-spell. The averaged self becomes the gift. That
reversal is the wizard's whole art — and the reason a concept and its mirror
are ONE entry with two directions, not two entries.

## The six

### 1. The Terrain — *enclosure ⇄ commons*
The living world and the one axis everything moves along. To enclose is to
capture the way to a thing — stand in the doorway, charge at the gate. To
common is to hold it open and tend it together.
*Cognates:* enclosure, commons, the knowing between us, the pre-individual field.

### 2. Incantation — *the spell ⇄ the counter-spell*
The casting of subjectivity. What it casts is **enchantment** — the felt
illumination of connection. Toward enclosure, the spell: disenchantment, common
sense, the self that believes it stands alone. Toward the commons, the
counter-spell: naming a thing so you see it coming, and the room changes. One
craft, two directions.
*Cognates:* incantation, enchantment, disenchantment, re-enchantment, hegemony,
naming, ideology, interpellation.

### 3. Magic — *the average ⇄ the singular*
The unique transformative capacity of singularities acting on each other. In a
person it is **the gift** — your magic, the one vantage that is yours alone. In
the land it is **the secret ways** — the magic of place, the paths and cracks
no grid can capture, the route around the door. The averaging machine reduces
singularities to interchangeable units, and where there is no singularity there
is no magic. The wizard restores the conditions for singularities to meet and act.
*Cognates:* singularity, intersectionality, exodus, deterritorialization, dérive,
transindividuation.

### 4. The Grimoire
A working list of what acts on you, drawn so you can act back. Each force it
names is a **demon** — not a devil but a power with a nature, a weakness, a use.
The book in your hands is one.
*Cognates:* theory/analysis as a tool for praxis; the demon.

### 5. The Wizard
One who has walked the terrain long enough to know it when it rises again, and
reaches to be useful: reads the field, wields the counter-spell, carries a gift,
knows the secret ways. Not a robe or a tower — a knowing held low, in the hands.
The capacities: name, read, build, hold.
*Cognates:* the organic intellectual; the radicalized subject.

### 6. Initiation
The walk by which one becomes a wizard; it changes what you can bear to see,
with no graduation. You were initiated once already — into the spell. This is
the second. No station last; the wizard returns capable, never home.
*Cognates:* radicalization; praxis as ongoing; conscientization.
