## The frame

The book's proof chapter (Ch 4) closes triumphant — G-04-025: *"It works. It
has been working in hundreds of places, across centuries."* The hope is asserted.
Following Hardt & Negri (already central to the book), every form of the commons
has a corrupted form; hope is *earned* only when the book shows the shadow:

- **Capture from without** — re-enclosure. The commons is taken back: bought,
  zoned, regulated, or starved out.
- **Corruption from within** — the commons hardens, narrows, or reproduces the
  thing it opposed (the co-op that becomes a clique; the commons that turns
  exclusive; the movement that ossifies).

The seam is already in the lexicon: the **deterritorialize-then-reterritorialize
discipline** — free a space, fail to re-common it, and it becomes the next
enclosure ("a thing freed too fast becomes the next thing for sale," now seeded
in G-GR-005, the grimoire legend).

## Key finding — the book already has a toehold

`G-04-008` ("Mondragón Now with Failures") already names real strains: Fagor
Electrodomésticos' 2013 bankruptcy (5,600 workers), cooperatives leaving the
federation in 2008 and 2022. But it frames them as **resilience** ("95% absorbed
within weeks"; "no member-worker laid off in 69 years"). So part of this work is
*re-weighting* an existing honest moment toward genuine pressure — not writing
from scratch.

2. **A new interlude** — "The Corruption of the Common," its own short space

4. **Complicate each site in place** — a one-beat shadow appended to each

## The sites + the questions to bring

For each: is there a shadow you want to show? From **without** (re-enclosure) or
**within** (corruption)? What do you know / can we source?

- **Mondragón** (G-04-008, G-13-026 Caja Laboral). Toehold exists. The commonly
  raised critique to confirm-or-reject with you: the global subsidiaries and
  non-member / temporary labor — does the federation's growth reproduce the wage
  relation it abolished at home? Your call whether that is the shadow, and your
  source.
- **Champlain Housing Trust** (G-14-008). The resale covenant caps speculation —
  but does it also cap wealth-building for the owner who most needs it? Is the
  shadow here a tension (equity vs. permanence), not a capture?
- **Cooperation Jackson** (G-04-018a/b). Young (10 years). Shadow may be fragility
  / scale / state hostility (capture-from-without) rather than internal
  corruption. What pressure is real here?
- **Kerala** (G-04-025). Seventy years of left governance — what are its
  enclosures (ecological, caste, out-migration / remittance dependence)? Your
  knowledge.
- **The Zapatistas** (G-04-025) — "restructured twice in their own lifetime."
  The restructuring may itself be the *answer* to corruption-from-within (the
  commons that re-commons itself). Candidate positive counter-example.
- **The digital commons / GPL / the web** (G-16-053/056). The clearest capture:
  the open web enclosed by platforms; open-source labor harvested into
  proprietary value (ties straight to 16b, the training corpus). Strong site —
  the capture is well-documented and recent.
