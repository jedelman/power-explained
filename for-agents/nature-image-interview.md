# Nature-Image Interview — images for the theory sections

**What this is.** The blind witnesses cool at every imageless theory section
and warm at every nature image — and when a nature image *explains* (the soil
absorbing leaves as capture), they think WITH it afterward ("they composted
it"). Jason's proposal (2026-06-10): a pass that weaves nature imagery into
the theory — Jason supplies the images (his brain, his provenance — the
Tier-3 constraint solved at the source), Claude weaves them under the full
critique + witness loop. This file is the interview: one row per theory
section, in river order, accumulated as the alchemical pass walks. Answer
inline (StackEdit-friendly: write under each **JASON:** prompt).

**The evidence so far (why images, specifically nature images):**
- Capture explained as soil absorbing fallen leaves → the re-witnessed ch-2
  reader's own words: "nobody stole my participation, they composted it."
  The image became the reader's thinking tool. That is explanation.
- The orchard/tree passage explaining biopower (Goetia) and Kropotkin's
  horses circling in the blizzard (ch 11) — theory AS image; no reader will
  ever call those passages a lecture.
- Ch-1's Gramsci passage HAS a metaphor — "operating systems are invisible
  until they crash" — and still half-chilled the witness ("the voice of
  someone who has read the right books"). Machine metaphors reach the mind;
  the witnesses report nature images landing "in the body before the mind."
- The mycorrhizal network carrying Simondon (ch 16/16b) is the existing
  proof at full scale.

**The discipline (from the sneaked-name cycle's lessons):**
- An IMAGE-MAP like the word-map: one image-family per concept, held
  consistently across the book; no collisions (ch-2's soil briefly meant
  both capture and fertile ground — a reader had to re-steady).
- Already-reserved image-families (do not reassign): herons = persistence /
  routing around the enclosure; mycorrhizae = the invisible commons /
  pre-individual field; the meadow = the unmown refusal; the cedar chip =
  witness/sigil; soil-absorbing = capture; the typhoon/dam = rebuilding
  commons; old-growth = the unenclosed; the deer = the body remembering.
- The image must BE the explanation (inhabited, extended, like the orchard),
  never an attached simile ("hegemony is like…").
- Format: Jason's image, Claude's weave, fresh witness verifies the section
  no longer chills. The interview answer can be a sentence or a paragraph —
  raw is fine; the weave is the centaur's job; provenance is the point.
