## VERBATIM REPORT

### Part A — Overall
It landed. Hard, in places. I came in braced to be lectured and I wasn't — that's the thing I
keep coming back to. The book trusts me. It gives me a deer at the edge of a clearing and
twenty five-year-olds dropping into a stalking gait before it gives me a single theory word,
and by the time the word arrives ("terrain," "the commons") it's landing into a hole the
feeling already dug. That's the move, over and over, and it mostly works on me.

The thinker stuff didn't make me feel dumb. Luxemburg smiling in the courtroom, Reich's six
tons of books burned, Turing's half-eaten apple, the cook written into the law — these are
*people* and *scenes* first, names second. I leaned in.

Where I bounced or skimmed:
- Part III's middle ("The Colony," "No Name for It") is the densest stretch, and it's where I
  most felt the book stack four thinkers per chapter. I never felt lectured, but I felt the
  reading slow from "thought" to "syllabus." It's the one place the book's own warning strains.
- The Barcelona chapter ("Two Cities") has a long stretch on the 1978 Spanish Constitution
  that went a bit policy-wonky after the gorgeous salsa-in-the-street opening.
- One tiny cringe: "You wonderful weird birds" is *almost* too much — it works because it's
  earned by 80k words, but it's right at the edge. It cashes out. Barely.

### Part B — Incomplete Threads

#### APPETITE — "Yes please, more of this"
1. **The bird-language / interspecies-reading thread.** "As we tried to understand them, they
   understood us." / "The crow cloud over the barred owl was one moment — the discovery that a
   feeling could be transmitted across species." The most ALIVE the book ever is. The one place
   "magic" stops being metaphor and becomes a literal practiced skill I could try tomorrow.
   **Pull: can't-stop-thinking-about-it.** More scenes, more of the learning curve, more of
   what it costs and opens.
2. **"What is your medicine?" / the woods community** (Orgone Accumulator). "what is your
   medicine?" The community that shakes armor loose through singing and moving — belonging shown
   not told. It glows and vanishes behind "which I will protect by not naming." **Pull: strong.**
3. **The "you can be one of us instead" restaurant as a scene.** "What we offered was: you can
   be one of us instead. The queers kept us in business." The whole book's promise in one
   sentence. I wanted to be in that room for a night the way I got to be in the woods camp.
   **Pull: strong.**
4. **Kokoro and the dojo.** "I have won fights I should have lost… on spirit, not skill." Armor
   (rigidity-from-fear) vs discipline (kept coordination). **Pull: mild-to-strong.**

#### GAPS — "Hey, where's the rest?"
1. **The young man "produced for an enclosure."** "I was trying to offer a commons to someone
   who had been produced for an enclosure… He was waiting for the clipboard. I had refused to
   lift it." The most haunting human portrait — the one person the framework failed to reach —
   and the thread ends in the author's exhaustion. Gestured at in Part VI ("the kid down the
   block whose system did not let them build a self") but never reconnected.
   **Pull: can't-stop-thinking-about-it.**
2. **"We'll meet her again" — Federici / the Witch.** "The Witch is the unkilled remainder of
   that capacity. She lives in everyone." We meet Federici again, but the witch-in-everyone
   never gets its payoff scene (a specific person's witch reawakening). Stays theory.
   **Pull: strong.**
3. **The Armed Forces Brewing vote — no resolution of the feeling.** Opens the book, recurs in
   You Wonderful Weird Birds; the brewery "went out of business about a year after it opened" is
   treated as a throwaway. Did the neighborhood's refusal contribute? Was there a buried win?
   The book returns to the wound (the board's cowardice), never to the possible victory.
   **Pull: strong.**
4. **The "I cannot deprogram debt calculation" admission.** "I can deprogram white supremacy
   out of my friends. I cannot deprogram debt calculation… the hardest thing to actually feel
   in your body as true." A stunning confession of a limit, then "the credit commons is already
   running" and move on. The deepest spell gets the least experiential treatment. **Pull: strong.**
5. **Erin.** "I notice systems / Erin notices space" — the co-perceiver, load-bearing for the
   theory of singularity-meeting-singularity ("It requires the difference between us to
   function"), and almost never a person in her own words. **Pull: strong.**
6. **"Come find us" / "the networks exist, you follow the thread."** Ends on "Come find us."
   But how? The book is specific about everything except the one practical thing a hungry reader
   most wants: where are these people, actually? **Pull: can't-stop-thinking-about-it.**
7. **Plotkin's "the soul's gift is not for the soul that carries it" — repeated, never enacted.**
   Appears 4×; the book flags "no chapter can say it once." But it never *shows* a specific
   person return from a descent and have their gift received by a waiting community. Neo
   diagnoses the *missing* return; the *successful* return never gets a scene. **Pull: strong.**
8. **The night herons "they know about the road… in ways we can't imagine."** The one knowing
   the book holds most sacred and won't try to read. Maybe that's the point. **Pull: mild-to-strong.**
9. **The tea room / nature school that never got built.** "The structures you do not build are
   part of the architecture too." Dropped in, never developed. **Pull: mild.**
10. **The seven-fifteen meeting.** The intro promises the book ends "in a room at seven-fifteen
    in the evening, with eleven people and an unread agenda. That's where the earth flowers."
    Recurs 3×, never *sat in*. The promised climactic scene is a pointer to a scene, not the
    scene. "I genuinely expected to walk into that room by the end. I never did."
    **Pull: can't-stop-thinking-about-it.**
