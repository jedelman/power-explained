   No Name for It** (+ **The Smile**) stacks 4+ thinkers/chapter on the heaviest material.
   Rule the reader named: *name + life + stakes carries; name + bare claim shuts out.*
   Approach = **HYBRID (Jason): thin, then story-ify.** Cut/demote the secondary names so

   treatment" (a body, a scene, a stake). **The thinning needs Jason's nod** — propose the

2. **Lecture-proof earlier/lighter.** The defusers ("not guilt — analysis"; the author

   chest. Move the "analysis, not an invoice — and I'm standing in it too" framing to the
   **front** of the Part III race material, before the four-thinker block.
3. **Cash out the Continental jargon (or cut).** "Hegemony/biopolitics/necropolitics/
   simulacra" all glossed-on-contact and landed. **"pre-individual field," "body without
   organs," "deterritorialization"** stayed cold — give them the concrete-image treatment
   (lean the mycorrhizal network all the way into "pre-individual field") or drop the
   French and keep the idea.
4. **Trim Part VI's case-study rhythm.** By the final third the pattern (thinker →
   commons → Norfolk parallel) is internalized; the reader skims examples to reach the
   personal beats (the floor, the cedar chip, the doves — always the payoff). Drop a case

   Wizards in the Woods**' abstract vocabulary-laying; it becomes a tool only once naming
   visibly changes something. Get one working "naming changes the room" spell in before
   the vocabulary.
