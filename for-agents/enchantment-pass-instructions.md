## The finding that reframes the pass

1. **The wizard's way is back-loaded.** Of the last seven chapters, four are
   wizard-register (Angel Faces Backward, Re-Enchanting, The Wizard's Walk, The
   Djinn, The Doves). The long analytical middle is the "disenchanted world,"
   and the wizard mostly arrives in detour-chapters to comment on it.

2. **The spine is ALREADY the wizard's way — unadmitted.** The prologue
   (G-PR-025–031) tells the reader the six Parts are not a syllabus but *"the
   oldest order the body knows — the one that runs every time a person picks up
   a trail"*: the feeling → the watching → the close work of the hands → what's
   shared with others → the elder's question → the return. **That is the
   tracker's path. That is the wizard's way.** It is already the load-bearing
   structure; the book just doesn't say so — the Parts wear analytical labels
   ("The Proof," "Why the System Is the Way It Is"), and the wizard's way is
   taught in detours instead of *being* the road.

3. **The Part metadata has drifted** and must be re-cut regardless: "Part V" is
   stamped on Re-Enchanting (position 21, mid-Part-VI); labels are inconsistent
   (`Part II:` vs `Part II —`); several chapters carry out-of-sequence parts.
   The clean re-cut is the natural carrier for this pass.

**So "pull the material forward" is not adding wizard content to analytical
chapters. It is revealing that the argument already IS the wizard's way, and
letting that frame govern from page one.**

---

## The central tension — and its resolution

The previous pass argued *for* the disenchanted middle (keep the wizard quiet
in the analytical chapters so reenchantment lands). This pass pushes the other
way. They reconcile by separating two things the book currently fuses:

- **Method** — *read the terrain → name the demon → find the magic* (the
  singular that resists the averaging machine). This SHOULD run throughout,
  demonstrated in every chapter. Present from the start.
- **Identity** — *"you are a wizard."* This stays a **reveal**. The reader
  practices the method for ~250 pages before being told that is what they were
  doing.

**Enchant the method throughout; save the naming of the reader for the end.**
This keeps Re-Enchanting's payoff intact while making the whole river
demonstrate the way. The disenchant→reenchant *content* arc can stay felt even
as the *method* enchants from gesture one (the overture already front-loads it;
the singular drumbeat and the early disenchantment beat were just restored —
see below).

## The three moves (recommended work-order)

### Move 1 — Re-spine the Parts as the stages of initiation  *(do first)*
Rename/reframe Parts I–VI from analytical labels to the tracker's six movements
the prologue already names. Reversible framing, not reorder; the scaffolding
exists. Lowest risk, highest leverage — and it tells us whether the wizard-spine
holds before anything moves.

Candidate spine (from G-PR-026–031 — confirm wording with Jason):
| Part | current label | the body's movement (PR-026–031) |
|---|---|---|
| I  | The Feeling Is Right | **The Feeling** — the tightness before thought |
| II | The Proof | **The Watching** — the body gathers evidence |
| III| Why the System Is the Way It Is | **The Hands** — close work: holding, taking apart |
| IV | Why the Obvious Fixes Failed | **The Telling** — what is shared with others |
| V  | The World the Framework Missed | **The Question** — the elder's turn |
| VI | What to Build | **The Return** — waking with it, building |

(The current six Parts and the six body-movements do not map cleanly today —
part of Move 1 is making them line up, and fixing the drifted metadata.)

### Move 2 — Rebalance the wizard chapters forward  *(do LAST; Jason moves order)*
Not random reordering. The candidate principle: each wizard chapter becomes the
**hinge that closes its Part** (the initiation at the end of each movement),
rather than clustering at the end of the book. Only after Move 1's framing
proves out, and only with Jason driving each relocation. **Chapter order is
sacred; the agent proposes, Jason moves.**

### Move 3 — Weave the read→name→magic move into each analytical chapter
The Goetia (P-GC, *The Speaking of Names*) teaches the method; every analytical
chapter then **practices** it: open by reading the terrain, name the demon at
work (most already do implicitly — the Colony names racial capitalism, ch10
names the outside), close on the magic that resists. The big composition pass,
done **one chapter at a time through the critique loop** (composition-pass
contract). Sequence it Part by Part, after Move 1.

**Recommended order of work: Move 1 → Move 3 (Part by Part) → Move 2.**
