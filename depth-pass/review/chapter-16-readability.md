## Notes

This is one of the stronger chapters in the book. The prose is rhythmically controlled throughout: the short declarative sentences ("The knowledge was useful. The person was a problem.") land hard against the longer analytical passages, and the emotional arc from intimate opening to systemic analysis to historical cases to the commons argument is handled without visible seams.

The readability intervention is minimal (one word) because the chapter earns its length. The grammar is clean, pronoun references are almost always clear, transitions follow the logic without signposting it, and the sentence-level variety is consistent with the style guide.

The inline CORRECTION and FLAG markers interrupt reading flow but are required artifacts of the correctness pass. They should be resolved before final publication.

One pattern worth noting for the author: the Tolkien digression (paragraph beginning "This is not unlike what Tolkien was working out...") is the chapter's most speculative move. It works, but it's doing something different from the rest of the chapter — importing an analogy from fiction rather than deriving it from the cases in hand. Whether that register shift is an asset or a risk is a judgment call for Jason, not a readability fix.
