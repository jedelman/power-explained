## Confirmed Corrections

| Location | Original | Corrected | Reason |

| Graeber paragraph, sentence 1 | "WTO protests in Quebec City in 2001" | "Summit of the Americas protests in Quebec City in 2001" | The Quebec City 2001 protests were against the 3rd Summit of the Americas (FTAA/Free Trade Area of the Americas negotiations), not the WTO. ## Flags for Human Review

| Location | Claim | Uncertainty |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| Kropotkin section, para 3 | Huxley "argued that nature was a 'gladiatorial show'" and that this was adopted by Social Darwinists | Accurate as far as it goes: Huxley's 1888 essay "The Struggle for Existence and Its Bearing Upon Man" did describe nature as gladiatorial and was Kropotkin's direct target. However, Huxley's later "Evolution and Ethics" (1893) explicitly *repudiated* Social Darwinism and argued that ethical society must *combat* nature's gladiatorial logic, not imitate it. The text risks implying Huxley endorsed Social Darwinism when he actually opposed applying it. The framing is a common simplification in Kropotkin scholarship. Recommend clarifying that Huxley described nature in those terms without endorsing Social Darwinism, or leaving the text as-is if the chapter intends a Kropotkin-POV reading rather than a neutral historical account. |
| Graeber paragraph, sentence 2 | "He helped coin the slogan 'We are the 99 percent'" | Wikipedia and Bloomberg describe Graeber as "sometimes credited" with coining the slogan; attribution is disputed. The chapter's phrasing ("helped coin") is defensible but should be flagged for author confirmation of confidence level. |
| Zapatista section, para 2 | "govern roughly a quarter of Chiapas" | Wikipedia and Schools for Chiapas sources say "about a third" of Chiapas territory, not a quarter. "A quarter" may understate the territory. Note: Zapatista territorial organization also changed significantly in 2023 (dissolution of MAREZ). Verify current figures and whether the "thirty years later" framing holds given 2023 reorganization. |

## Checked and Clear

- **Kropotkin biographical facts**: Born Moscow 1842. Descended from Rurik dynasty (through father). Educated at the Corps of Pages. Appointed personal page to Tsar Alexander II. Verified against Wikipedia, Britannica, Embryo Project Encyclopedia.
- **Kropotkin's Siberian fieldwork**: Five years in the 1860s (1863–1867) across Siberia and Manchuria, mapping glaciers and geology. Confirmed accurate.
- **Mutual Aid: A Factor of Evolution**: Publication year 1902 confirmed. Title exact. Essays originally serialized in *The Nineteenth Century* 1890–1896. Kropotkin's argument about cooperation vs. competition confirmed.
- **Bookchin biography**: Born in the Bronx in 1921. Son of Jewish immigrant parents (from Russian Empire). Social Ecology theory attribution confirmed.
- **Cross Bronx Expressway displacement**: "sixty thousand people" — sources support this figure (some cite 1,500 as directly displaced from the corridor itself, with ~60,000 total relocated during the full expressway construction period 1948–1972 per Robert Caro and others). The text's figure is consistent with the commonly cited broader displacement.
- **Atlantic City (Norfolk, VA)**: Confirmed as a real Norfolk neighborhood destroyed by urban renewal — highways and a medical complex replaced it. The parallel Moses/Hampton Boulevard construction in the text reads as a rhetorical comparison (Moses AND the Norfolk planners), not an attribution of Hampton Boulevard to Moses. This appears intentional.
- **Öcalan capture and imprisonment**: Captured 1999. Imprisoned on Imrali Island. Confirmed accurate.
- **Zapatista uprising date**: January 1, 1994, the day NAFTA took effect. Confirmed accurate.
- **Caracoles and Juntas de Buen Gobierno**: Names, translation ("Good Government Councils"), rotating non-salaried governance structure. All confirmed accurate.
- **Subcomandante Marcos**: Name and role as EZLN spokesman and communiqué writer confirmed.
- **Graeber death**: Venice, September 2020, age 59. Confirmed (died September 2, 2020).
- **Graeber visit to Rojava**: Confirmed — he visited and wrote a foreword to *Revolution in Rojava*; LSE interview confirmed.
- **Rojava population**: Text says "four million people" — sources cite approximately 4.6 million; "four million" is a reasonable approximation, not an error.
- **Bookchin → Öcalan connection**: Öcalan read Bookchin in prison; Bookchin's Social Ecology influenced democratic confederalism. Confirmed.
- **Three primordial economic forms (Graeber)**: Communism, Exchange, Hierarchy — confirmed as Graeber's framework from *Debt: The First 5,000 Years*.
- **Graeber on play**: Framework attribution is consistent with Graeber's published work.
- **Gramsci "war of position"**: The concept is accurately described and the term correctly attributed to Gramsci.
- **Barcelona superblocks**: City program confirmed. The re-naturalization of streets (trees, permeable surfaces, planting) is confirmed. "Superblocks" is the common English translation of "superilles/superblocks."

## Notes

The chapter is factually solid on its main biographical and historical claims. The one confirmed correction (Quebec City/WTO vs. Summit of the Americas) is a common misremembering — the "anti-globalization" protest sequence of 1999–2001 saw multiple targets (WTO in Seattle 1999, IMF/World Bank in Prague 2000, Summit of the Americas in Quebec City 2001) and the labels are frequently conflated.

The Huxley flag is the most substantively important: the text uses Huxley's gladiatorial language in a way that could mislead readers about his actual politics. Whether to revise depends on whether the chapter is presenting Kropotkin's own framing (in which case simplifying Huxley is defensible) or making an independent historical claim (in which case a nuance is warranted).

The Zapatista territorial figure should be verified — the 2023 dissolution of the formal MAREZ structure also complicates the "thirty years later" framing and may warrant an update.
