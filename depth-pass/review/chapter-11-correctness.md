## Flags for Human Review

| Kropotkin section, para 3 | Huxley "argued that nature was a 'gladiatorial show'" — implies Huxley endorsed Social Darwinism | Huxley's 1888 essay "The Struggle for Existence and Its Bearing Upon Man" did describe nature as gladiatorial, and Kropotkin responded directly to it. Huxley was not a Social Darwinist. The text's framing is a common simplification in Kropotkin scholarship but could mislead readers about Huxley's actual position. ## Checked and Clear

- **Kropotkin biographical facts**: Born Moscow 1842. Descended from Rurik dynasty (through father). Educated at the Corps of Pages. Appointed personal page to Tsar Alexander II. Verified against Wikipedia, Britannica, Embryo Project Encyclopedia.
- **Kropotkin's Siberian fieldwork**: Five years in the 1860s (1863–1867) across Siberia and Manchuria, mapping glaciers and geology. Confirmed accurate.
- **Mutual Aid: A Factor of Evolution**: Publication year 1902 confirmed. Title exact. Essays originally serialized in *The Nineteenth Century* 1890–1896. Argument about cooperation confirmed.
- **Bookchin biography**: Born in the Bronx in 1921. Son of Jewish immigrant parents (from Russian Empire). Social Ecology theory attribution confirmed.
- **Cross Bronx Expressway displacement**: "sixty thousand people" — consistent with broadly cited figures (approximately 60,000 total displaced during full construction period 1948–1972, per Robert Caro and others).
- **Atlantic City (Norfolk, VA)**: Confirmed real Norfolk neighborhood destroyed by urban renewal — highways and medical complex replaced it. The text's parallel construction ("Moses AND the planners who built Hampton Boulevard over Atlantic City") reads as rhetorical comparison, not attribution of Hampton Boulevard to Moses. Intentional.
- **Öcalan capture and imprisonment**: Captured 1999. Imprisoned on Imrali Island. Confirmed accurate.
- **Zapatista uprising date**: January 1, 1994, the day NAFTA took effect. Confirmed accurate.
- **Caracoles and Juntas de Buen Gobierno**: Names, translation ("Good Government Councils"), rotating non-salaried governance structure. Confirmed accurate.
- **Subcomandante Marcos**: Name and role as EZLN spokesman and communiqué writer confirmed.
- **Graeber death**: Venice, September 2020, age 59. Confirmed (died September 2, 2020).
- **Graeber visit to Rojava**: Confirmed — he visited and wrote foreword to *Revolution in Rojava*; LSE video interview on record.
- **Rojava population**: Text says "four million people" — sources cite approximately 4.6 million; "four million" is a reasonable approximation.
- **Bookchin → Öcalan connection**: Öcalan read Bookchin in prison; Social Ecology influenced democratic confederalism. Confirmed.
- **Three primordial economic forms (Graeber)**: Communism, Exchange, Hierarchy — confirmed as Graeber's framework from *Debt: The First 5,000 Years*.
- **Gramsci "war of position"**: Concept accurately described and correctly attributed.
- **Barcelona superblocks**: Program confirmed. Re-naturalization of streets (trees, permeable surfaces) confirmed.

## Notes

The chapter is factually solid on its main biographical and historical claims. The one confirmed correction (Quebec City/WTO vs. Summit of the Americas) is a common conflation — the anti-globalization sequence of 1999–2001 targeted the WTO in Seattle (1999), IMF/World Bank in Prague (2000), and the Summit of the Americas in Quebec City (2001), and the labels get blurred.

The Huxley flag is the most substantively important: the text implies Huxley was a proponent of Social Darwinism when he explicitly opposed it. Whether to revise depends on the chapter's intent. If presenting Kropotkin's own polemical framing, simplifying Huxley is defensible. If making an independent historical claim, a nuance is needed.

The Zapatista territorial figure should be verified against current sources given the 2023 reorganization.
