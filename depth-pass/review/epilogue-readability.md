# Readability Pass: The Doves (Epilogue)

## Changes Made

| Location | Original | Revised | Reason |
|----------|----------|---------|--------|
| Para 2 (kinglet section) | "Every morning it's still there is a small triumph." | "Every morning it's still there, a small triumph." | The implicit relative clause ("every morning [that] it's still there") causes a real parsing stumble. Without the comma, "is a small triumph" appears to conflict with "still there." Adding the comma resolves the ambiguity immediately. Meaning and rhythm preserved. |

## Considered but Left

| Location | Element | Why Left |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Para 5 ("The night herons know...") | Near-verbatim repetition of lines from para 2: "their territory maps include the crossing, the people who bless the nests are part of what they know about this place." | This language appears almost identically in both the night heron section (para 2) and the final accumulation paragraph (para 5). Could read as inadvertent repetition. Left because: (1) it may be deliberate — a structural callback that completes a circle; (2) removing or altering it would constitute removing content. Author should decide whether the echo is intentional. |
| Para 3 ("Recovery doesn't come back the same way.") | "come back" applied to "recovery" is slightly awkward | Left because the meaning is clear in context and the phrase may be intentionally colloquial — the point is that what returns doesn't resemble what was lost. No real reader would stumble here. |
| Para 1 (kinglet) | FLAG marker mid-sentence disrupts reading flow | Cannot touch FLAG markers per pass rules. The disruption is a consequence of the inline flagging convention, not a prose deficiency. |
| Para 4 ("For the particular quality of a summer evening...") | Long anaphoric sentence with three "and" clauses | Left. This is deliberate parallel rhythm — cutting or restructuring it would alter a rhetorical structure. |
| "5am" (final awareness section) | Not hyphenated or punctuated as "5 a.m." | Left. Informal rendering is consistent with the prose voice and unlikely to cause a stumble. |

## Notes

The epilogue is among the tightest prose in the book. Voice is consistent throughout: direct, grounded, emotionally honest. Very little redundancy. The only real reader-stumble was the "Every morning it's still there is a small triumph" construction. One change made.

The near-verbatim repetition of the night heron territory map language across two distant paragraphs is worth the author's attention. If it is intentional (structural echo closing the book), it works. If it arrived through revision drift, one of the two instances should be varied or cut.
