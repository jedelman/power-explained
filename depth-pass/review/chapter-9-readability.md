# Readability Pass: Chapter 9 — He Built It Anyway

## Changes Made

| Location | Original | Revised | Reason |
|----------|----------|---------|--------|
| Para opening the collapse section | "The national socialist party leadership, afraid of where it was going, refused to extend the action nationally." | "The Italian Socialist Party leadership, afraid of where it was going, refused to extend the action nationally." | "national socialist party" is a generic lowercase descriptor that in context (Italian fascism, Mussolini) risks being read as a reference to the Nazi Party ("National Socialist" is the Nazi party's name). A real reader would stumble. Capitalizing and specifying "Italian Socialist Party" removes the ambiguity without changing meaning. |
| Opening of penultimate paragraph (close section) | "But the builders have not stopped." | "The builders have not stopped." | Two consecutive paragraphs begin with "But" — the nature paragraph ("But the great crested flycatchers...") and this one. Both are contrastive but they contrast against different things, and the doubled opening creates momentary confusion about what the second "But" is conceding against. Removing it tightens the turn and the declarative lands harder. |

## Notes

The prose in Chapter 9 is among the strongest in the book. The emotional arc is well-controlled: the concrete historical opening, the turn to hegemony as concept, the personal restaurant passage, the examples, the close. Sentence rhythm is varied and effective throughout — the deliberate fragments ("Not of will or organization or courage. Of territory.") do real work.

The only genuine clarity issue was the double "But" opening consecutive paragraphs in the closing section, and the "national socialist party" phrasing that risks a false cognate with the Nazi party. Both addressed.

The inline CORRECTION and FLAG markers from the correctness pass remain visible in the chapter file. These should be stripped or resolved before publication — they are review annotations, not prose.
