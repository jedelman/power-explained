## Notes

This chapter is among the stronger pieces in the manuscript. The prose is controlled, the voice is consistent, and the rhythm varies well — the short declarative punches ("Then it wasn't." / "Not charity. Crisis management." / "It was an organizing victory.") land as intended. The CDFI sentence was the only genuine syntactic stumble; everything else was a judgment call about style, not clarity.

The boomerang bottle passage is the strongest prose in the chapter — the extension of Graeber and Bourdieu through a local, specific, grounded example is exactly the technique that makes the theoretical material feel real. The close lands.

One structural note for author's consideration (not a readability intervention): the chapter's opening narrates the Rolling Jubilee action and then pivots to Graeber's argument, then returns to action via the Corinthian story. The return to action is well-handled. The Graeber section in the middle is doing a lot of explanatory work that slightly arrests the narrative momentum. This is a structural question, not a sentence-level one — noting it here only because it's the kind of thing that a future draft pass might address.
