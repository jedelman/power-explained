## Checked and Clear

- **Quercus phellos as willow oak**: Correct. *Quercus phellos* is the scientific name for willow oak. Leaves are long and narrow (to 5" long, 1" wide), turning yellow to russet/amber in fall; persist on tree into winter. All details in the text are accurate.
- **"not willows and oaks separately — a single species"**: Accurate. Willow oak is one species in the oak genus (Quercus) with willow-like leaves; the clarification is correct.
- **Gramsci, "common sense," and hegemony**: The text's framing is accurate. Gramsci distinguished "common sense" (senso comune) — the sediment of prior thought embedded in daily life — from critical philosophical consciousness. The contrast with "false consciousness" is a recognized interpretive distinction in Gramsci scholarship: common sense is not mere manipulation but the lived ideological medium through which hegemony operates. Attribution and characterization are correct.
- **Kinglet as "one of the smallest birds in North America"**: Accurate. Both North American kinglet species (ruby-crowned and golden-crowned) are consistently described as among the smallest passerines on the continent. Golden-crowned kinglet is approximately 3.25–4 inches; ruby-crowned 3.5–4.3 inches.
- **Night herons nesting in willow oaks in spring**: Consistent with documented behavior in Hampton Roads. Yellow-crowned night herons (the predominant Norfolk species) typically arrive and begin nesting in early spring (March–April). Nesting in willow oaks is plausible as local observation, though Center for Conservation Biology research notes the majority of yellow-crowned night heron nests in lower Tidewater, VA are in loblolly pines. Jason's direct observation of nesting in the willow oaks on this specific street is firsthand.
- **Robert's Rules of Order permitting floor introduction of new business**: Accurate. Under standard Robert's Rules, members may raise items under "new business" even if not on the prepared agenda; a motion from the floor requires a second and a majority vote to adopt.
- **Armed Forces Brewing Company**: name, nature (brewery seeking CUP), location in Norfolk, neighborhood character (LGBTQ-friendly, adjacent to LGBT Life Center), LGBT Life Center subsequently moving — all confirmed accurate by multiple local news sources (Virginia Mercury, WAVY, WTKR, VPM).
- **"majority-Black" neighborhood**: The Railroad District / Park Place area of Norfolk is historically majority-Black. Consistent with all coverage; could not independently confirm exact demographic breakdown.
