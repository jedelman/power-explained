## Notes

This chapter is the closing argument of the book. The prose reflects that: it is denser and more layered than earlier chapters, but the layering is structural rather than obscuring. Almost every element that might look like a stumble on first scan resolves as deliberate on close reading — fragments used for emphasis, repetition used for anaphora, long sentences that are internally ordered.

The one area an author might want to revisit is the Graeber paragraph (para 4), which packs the most complex clause structure. It holds, but it demands the most of a first-time reader. No change recommended — just awareness.

The readability of this chapter depends significantly on whether readers have met the thinkers named (Graeber, H&N, Deleuze/Guattari, Simondon, Plotkin, Prechtel, Ostrom, Federici) in earlier chapters. For a reader arriving cold, several paragraphs would feel opaque. That is a structural issue for the book as a whole, not a readability problem within this chapter.
