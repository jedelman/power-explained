## Checked and Clear

- **Centauromachy at Pirithous's wedding**: The chapter says the centaurs "tried to carry off the women." Confirmed — at Pirithous's wedding the centaurs attempted to abduct Hippodamia (the bride) and other women guests.
- **Chiron's students — Achilles, Heracles, Asclepius, Jason, Peleus**: All five confirmed as Chiron's students across multiple classical sources (Theoi, Wikipedia, Britannica, World History Encyclopedia).
- **Chiron's location — cave on Mount Pelion**: Confirmed. Multiple sources place Chiron's cave on Mount Pelion.
- **Heracles accidentally wounded Chiron with a Hydra-venom arrow**: Confirmed accurate. The arrow carried Hydra venom (from the Lernaean Hydra), was shot by Heracles, and struck Chiron accidentally.
- **Chiron surrendered immortality so Prometheus could be freed**: Confirmed. The exchange — Chiron relinquishing immortality so Zeus could free Prometheus — is attested in multiple classical sources.
- **Zeus placed Chiron in the stars**: Confirmed. Classical myth has Zeus honoring Chiron by placing him among the stars (associated with either the Centaurus or Sagittarius constellation, depending on the source; the chapter does not specify which).
- **Valencia water tribunal and "ten centuries"**: The tribunal dates to approximately 960 CE (founded during the reigns of Abd-ar-Rahman III and Al-Hakam II), making ten centuries accurate as of this writing.
- **Simondon's "pre-individual field"**: Confirmed. Gilbert Simondon's philosophy of individuation centers on pre-individual fields as the condition of possibility for individuation — the text's attribution is accurate.
- **Fanon's clinical practice in Algeria**: Confirmed. Fanon's "Colonial War and Mental Disorders" chapter in *The Wretched of the Earth* draws from his clinical work treating both colonized Algerians and French officers/police involved in colonial repression.
- **The Wretched of the Earth title**: Confirmed. Title is correct, attribution to Fanon is correct.
