## Checked and Clear

- **Baudrillard's *The System of Objects* (1968):** Year confirmed. Published 1968 as *Le Système des objets*, based on his 1966 doctoral thesis.
- **Guy Debord's *Society of the Spectacle* (1967):** Publication year confirmed as November 1967 (Buchet-Chastel). Connection to May '68 accurate — the events brought the book wider attention.
- **Situationist slogans quoted:** "Under the cobblestones, the beach," "Be realistic, demand the impossible," "It is forbidden to forbid" — all confirmed as slogans associated with May '68.
- **Alan Turing's 1936 paper:** Published in the *Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society*. Paper received May 1936, published November 1936/January 1937. Chapter's description as "a paper in a mathematics journal" is accurate if imprecise.
- **Turing as "almost Olympic level" runner:** Confirmed. His marathon time of 2:46:03 was 11 minutes off the 1948 Olympic winning time. He placed fifth in the 1948 Olympic trial. Sources describe him as "a fine athlete of almost Olympic standard."
- **Christopher Morcom died "suddenly in 1930":** Confirmed. Morcom died February 13, 1930, from complications of bovine tuberculosis.
- **Turing's 1952 prosecution:** Confirmed. Police discovered the relationship during investigation of a burglary. Both Turing and Arnold Murray were charged. Turing pleaded guilty. Offered choice between prison and hormonal injections; chose injections.
- **Turing died June 1954, aged forty-one, inquest ruled suicide:** All confirmed. Death by cyanide poisoning.
- **Bletchley Park, Enigma, Turing's role:** Confirmed. Turing led Hut 8 (naval Enigma), designed the bombe. The chapter's claim that he "led the team that... cracked the Enigma cipher" is broadly accurate; more precisely, he headed naval Enigma specifically. The "German Navy, the German Army, the German High Command" framing is a slight generalization but not a specific factual error.
- **"Historians believe this work shortened the war by at least two years":** Confirmed. Standard historical consensus places the figure at two to four years.
- **Stallman's GNU General Public License — 1989:** Confirmed. Version 1 released February 25, 1989.
- **Berners-Lee's 1989 proposal and "Vague but exciting":** Confirmed. Proposal submitted March 1989. Supervisor Mike Sendall wrote "Vague but exciting" on the cover page.
- **Linus Torvalds — "a Finnish student":** Confirmed. He was a computer science student at the University of Helsinki.
- **Torvalds's post — 1991:** Confirmed. Posted August 25, 1991 to comp.os.minix.
- **Linux shipped under the GPL:** Confirmed in substance (adopted GPLv2 with version 0.12, January 1992).
- **CERN released web into public domain — 1993:** Confirmed. April 30, 1993.
- **Stonewall Inn, June 1969:** Confirmed. June 28, 1969.
- **Marsha P. Johnson — Black, transgender, present at Stonewall:** Confirmed. She was 23 at the time. Her exact role in initiating the uprising is historically debated but her presence on the front lines is not disputed.
- **Törbel community, governance charter, 1483:** Confirmed. The 1483 treaty regulated collective use of meadow, forest, and water channels. Ostrom used Törbel as first case study in *Governing the Commons* (1990).
- **Silvia Federici — healers, midwives, executed as witches, knowledge extracted:** Confirmed as a core argument of *Caliban and the Witch* (2004). The chapter places this "in fifteenth-century Europe" — Federici's analysis covers the 15th–17th centuries; the 15th century is the starting point she identifies.
- **Gilles Deleuze, "body without organs," deterritorialization/territorialization:** Confirmed concepts developed by Deleuze (with Guattari) in *Anti-Oedipus* (1972) and *A Thousand Plateaus* (1980). The chapter attributes these to Deleuze "writing in the aftermath of 1968" — *Anti-Oedipus* was published 1972, consistent with that framing.
- **Gilbert Simondon, "pre-individual field":** Confirmed concept. Simondon's theory of individuation and the preindividual field is accurately characterized.
- **Michael Hardt and Antonio Negri on love as force:** Confirmed. Love as a political force is a central theme in their collaborative work, particularly *Empire* (2000) and *Commonwealth* (2009).
- **Berlin Wall fell in 1989:** Confirmed.

## Notes

The Debord date error (1967 vs. 1968) is easy to miss because the two books are so frequently discussed together in the context of May '68, and the chapter's framing implies contemporaneity. The correction changes only the bridging phrase, not Debord's argument or its relationship to May '68.

The Torvalds quote error is a single interpolated word ("anything") that does not change the meaning but is inaccurate as a direct quotation.

The "three weeks" claim is the most substantively uncertain item. Jason should verify his intended meaning before changing it — if the intent is specifically the period of acute street fighting and occupations (mid-May through early June), a more defensible phrase might be "weeks" or "a month."
