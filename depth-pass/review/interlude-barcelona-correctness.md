## Checked and Clear

- **El Born neighborhood**: Confirmed as the location of the Mercat de Santa Caterina (active food market with fish stalls) and Mercat del Born (converted to cultural center since 1971). The text refers to an unnamed active fish market in El Born — consistent with Mercat de Santa Caterina. No name is asserted, so no error.
- **1960s and 1970s car dominance**: Confirmed. Multiple sources document that Barcelona's Cerdà-grid streets, originally designed for trams and pedestrians, were overtaken by automobile traffic in the 1960s and 1970s.
- **Superblocks description — "clusters of streets deprioritized for through traffic"**: Accurate characterization. Confirmed by municipal documents and multiple urban planning sources.
- **"The policy was controversial when it started"**: Accurate. The Poblenou pilot (2016) and subsequent implementations faced significant local opposition from residents and businesses.
- **Reservoir levels below twenty percent**: Accurate. In 2024, average reservoir levels for Catalonia's internal basins fell to 14% (March 8, 2024 data). In 2008, levels reached 20.1%. The text's claim that reservoirs have been "some years below twenty percent of capacity" is supported by the record.
- **Mediterranean warming, precipitation pattern shifts**: Consistent with well-documented climate science for the region.
- **"The snowpack that feeds the rivers is thinner each decade"**: Confirmed. Pyrenees snowmelt feeds rivers (including the Ter) that supply Barcelona's reservoirs. Snowpack reduction under warming is documented in peer-reviewed literature and observed data.
