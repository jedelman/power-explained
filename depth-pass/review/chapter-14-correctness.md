## Checked and Clear

- **Atlantic City demolition / 1957**: Confirmed. Demolition began in 1957 to clear the neighborhood for Norfolk General Hospital and road infrastructure.
- **Champlain Housing Trust / Bernie Sanders / 1984**: Confirmed. The Burlington Community Land Trust (now Champlain Housing Trust) was seeded in 1984 by Sanders' administration with a $200,000 grant.
- **Brenda Torpy / led the trust for two decades**: Confirmed as approximately accurate. Torpy joined as Executive Director in 1991 after serving as founding Board President; she led the organization through the 2010s.
- **263 community land trusts in the United States**: Plausible for the writing period; verified counts range from ~260 (2020–21 figures) to 289 (2021) to 308 (2024). The specific number 263 falls within the range but could not be pinned to an exact source. Not flagged as wrong; contextually accurate.
- **Henry George / 1879**: Confirmed. *Progress and Poverty* was published in 1879.
- **Kayapó territory / "roughly 11 million hectares"**: Confirmed as approximately accurate. The five demarcated Kayapó territories total approximately 10.6–11.3 million hectares depending on source.
- **Kayapó deforestation lower than surrounding areas during Bolsonaro years**: Confirmed. Studies show deforestation inside Kayapó territory remained dramatically lower than in adjacent non-Indigenous lands, even as surrounding areas saw dramatic increases under the Bolsonaro administration.
- **Global Witness / "over 200 killings" / 2020 / majority in Latin America**: Confirmed. The 2020 report documented 227 killings; majority were in Latin America (Colombia, Mexico, Philippines were highest).
- **Tortuguita killed by police / January 2023**: Confirmed. Manuel Esteban Paez Terán (Tortuguita) was killed by Georgia State Patrol on January 18, 2023.
- **Weelaunee / Muscogee name**: Confirmed. Weelaunee is the Muscogee Creek name for the South River; the forest was dubbed Weelaunee Forest by community members and Muscogee representatives in 2021.
- **Indigenous and community-managed territories / roughly 22 percent of Earth's land surface**: Confirmed. Multiple authoritative sources (UN, Mongabay, WRI) cite 22 percent as the standard figure.
- **Forest Rights Act of 2006 / India**: Confirmed. The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act was passed in 2006.
- **David Graeber / *Debt***: Confirmed. Full title is *Debt: The First 5,000 Years* (2011); the shortened *Debt* reference in context is acceptable.
- **E. Richard Sorenson / anthropologist / New Guinea, Micronesia, Andaman Sea, South America**: Confirmed. Sorenson documented preconquest consciousness in communities in central New Guinea and the eastern Andaman Sea (Sea Nomads), among other regions.
- **Sorenson / cultural collapse from tourism on island in eastern Andaman Sea**: Confirmed. Sorenson described this collapse — occurring with the arrival of tourism — as a rapid and irreversible destruction of the liminal consciousness and community rapport.
- **Bill Plotkin / ecopsychologist**: Confirmed. Plotkin is the founder of the Animas Valley Institute and author of works on the "descent to soul" and nature-based soul initiation.
- **Standing Rock / 2016 / Dakota Access Pipeline / water cannons / rubber bullets / mass arrest**: Confirmed. All three state responses are documented.

## Notes

- The Kayapó claim is nuanced: during the Bolsonaro years, illegal mining significantly increased inside Kayapó territory and Kayapó lands appeared on some lists of most-affected Indigenous territories. The chapter's claim is specifically about deforestation rates relative to surrounding non-Indigenous areas — which is accurate. If the author wants to be more precise, they could note that illegal mining (not deforestation) was the primary threat during the Bolsonaro period inside Kayapó territory itself.
- The "old-growth forest" description of Weelaunee/South River Forest in Atlanta: technically the area is secondary forest on former prison farm land, not old-growth. However this characterization may be authorial/rhetorical rather than a strict botanical claim; left for author judgment.
