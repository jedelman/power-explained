# Readability Pass: They're Sprinkling Us In (Chapter 2)

## Changes Made
| Location | Original | Revised | Reason |
|----------|----------|---------|--------|
| Atlantic City description | "beneath what is now a medical campus" | "under what is now a medical campus" | "around... and beneath" created a visual confusion — the neighborhood is not beneath the ground, it's been built over. "Under" carries the same spatial sense more naturally in this context, and avoids the awkward double-"now" compound alongside "around what is now." |
| Forrest White attribution | "This was, as historian Forrest White has documented, a technique." | "Historian Forrest White has documented what this was: a technique." | The embedded parenthetical interrupted parsing of "This was... a technique." Readers had to hold the subject and predicate across the interjection. Restructuring keeps the emphasis on "technique" (the rhetorical payoff) while giving White clear attribution. Meaning unchanged. |
| "been sustaining itself" | "The slum was manufactured, and then used to justify destroying the community that had, until the manufacture began, been sustaining itself." | "The slum was manufactured, then used to justify destroying the community that had, until the manufacture began, sustained itself." | Two small cleanups: (1) dropped the comma before "and then" — the compound predicate doesn't need it; (2) "been sustaining itself" → "sustained itself" — the progressive form added no meaning and the sentence was already dense. |
| "everywhere designed to" | "participatory processes everywhere designed to absorb participation rather than transmit it into power" | "participatory processes designed to absorb participation rather than transmit it into power" | "everywhere" was syntactically ambiguous — it could modify "processes" (processes everywhere) or function adverbially (designed everywhere). The intended meaning (these processes exist broadly) was clear from context without the word, and its removal eliminates the stumble. |
| Closing refusal | "That refusal — the naming, the seeing clearly, the refusal to absorb the loss as progress — is not nothing." | "That refusal — the naming, the seeing clearly, the refusal to call the loss progress — is not nothing." | "Refusal" appeared twice in one sentence (once before the dash, once inside it). The second instance's phrasing "to absorb the loss as progress" also slightly muddied the meaning — she's refusing to *name* the loss as progress, not to absorb it. "To call the loss progress" is crisper and removes the stutter. |

## Notes
The prose is in good shape. The voice is consistent throughout: direct, controlled, not ornate. The opening vignette and closing section are both strong and needed no changes. The Atlantic City historical section carries the most load and was the most complex to read — partly due to the correctness flags now embedded in it, which will make it look rougher than it will read once those are resolved.

One observation for the author: the leverage paragraph (starting "There is a word for the version of this that works") runs somewhat long and the three-part anaphora ("that's leverage... that's leverage... that's leverage") is effective but the third example ("the neighborhood that organized around speed bumps") is the weakest of the three. The speed bumps example is less legible as a commons/leverage argument than the other two. This is a content note, not a prose note — left untouched per the mandate.

The sentence "Ask it of everything: Does engaging with this produce relationships, capacity, knowledge that persist beyond the specific campaign?" is doing a lot and the colon + three-question structure is slightly unusual. It works. Left.
