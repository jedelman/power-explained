## Notes

This chapter is in strong shape. The prose has genuine shape: the opening Ilocos sequence earns its weight, the turn through Hardin to Ostrom is cleanly executed, and the Foucault/Negri section lands the theoretical payload without becoming lecture. The personal Norfolk section is brief and earns its place.

The one change made (removing the "actual/actually" redundancy in the Ostrom paragraph) is the only place a real reader would reliably stumble. Everything else that reads as unusual is deliberate — fragment lists, chiasmus, compressed syntax, voice-specific diction.

The confirmed correction to the Negri paragraph (Empire was not written in prison) will require the author to decide how to reintegrate that passage once the flags are resolved. The current corrected draft is factually accurate but the rhetorical connection to Gramsci-as-prison-writer is partially severed. The author may want to restore that thread by referencing Negri's earlier prison writings explicitly, or by reframing the Gramsci parallel around intellectual lineage rather than shared incarceration.

Word count is well-controlled. No bullet lists exceeded three items. No definition sentences. Voice is consistent throughout.
