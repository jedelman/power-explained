## Checked and Clear

- **Rosa Luxemburg / birds from prison**: Confirmed. Luxemburg wrote extensively about birds in her prison letters (1916–18, from Wronke and Breslau), identifying species by name with taxonomic precision. The characterization "naming birds through prison bars" is accurate and well-documented.
- **Rosa Luxemburg as historical figure**: Confirmed. Born 5 March 1871, murdered 15 January 1919. Revolutionary socialist theorist, co-founder of the Spartacus League and KPD.
- **Paulo Freire**: Confirmed. Born 19 September 1921, died 2 May 1997. *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* written 1967–68, first published in Spanish 1968, English translation 1970.
- **Kinglet as "one of the smallest birds in North America"**: Confirmed. Kinglets are among the smallest passerines in North America, second only to hummingbirds in overall small-bird rankings.
- **Night herons / "up from Central America"**: Confirmed. Black-crowned night herons wintering in Central America and returning north in spring is consistent with documented migration data (banded birds recovered in Mexico, Central America, and the Caribbean).
- **Night herons nesting in willow oaks**: Plausible. Night herons nest in a variety of oak and other tree species. Willow oaks are common in the Mid-Atlantic/Southeast. No specific source found for willow oaks specifically, but no reason to flag as incorrect — this appears to be the author's direct local observation, not a general claim.

## Notes

The epilogue makes few hard factual claims. The main correctness item was the punctuation error in "twenty five-year-olds." The inline flag on kinglet size (already present before this pass) is accurate and was left untouched.
