## Checked and Clear

- **The Matrix (1999)**: Film year, directors, the hollow-book scene using *Simulacra and Simulation* as the hiding place — all confirmed accurate.
- **Wachowski trans identities**: Confirmed. Both Lana and Lilly Wachowski are trans women. Lana publicly came out in 2012, Lilly in 2016; neither was publicly out at the time of *The Matrix*'s release.
- **Baudrillard's four stages of the image**: The chapter's summary (reflects real → distorts → masks absence → precedes/produces the real) accurately follows Baudrillard's own formulation in *Simulacra and Simulation*.
- **RiceTec Basmati patent**: Confirmed Texas-based company; patent granted 1997; significant claims subsequently invalidated after Indian government challenge (2000–2002). The chapter's language ("Some claims were eventually invalidated after legal challenge") is accurate and appropriately hedged.
- **GPL year**: Stallman wrote the GPL in 1989 — confirmed (first published February 25, 1989).
- **Berners-Lee web proposal 1989**: Confirmed (March 1989, submitted to CERN management).
- **Torvalds 1991**: Confirmed (announcement posted August 25, 1991).
- **Public domain release 1993**: Confirmed (CERN put WWW code in public domain April 30, 1993).
- **Norbert Wiener coined "cybernetics"**: Confirmed. Published *Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine* in 1948. Term coined 1947 with Arturo Rosenblueth.
- **Etymology of *kybernetes***: Confirmed as "steersman/helmsman" from Greek. The chapter's gloss ("the one who steers not by overpowering the water but by reading it") is interpretive elaboration, not a factual claim — left as-is.
- **Sylvia Wynter "overrepresentation of Man"**: Attribution confirmed. Wynter's key essay "Unsettling the Coloniality of Being/Power/Truth/Freedom: Towards the Human, After Man, Its Overrepresentation — An Argument" (2003) uses this exact formulation.
- **Simondon "pre-individual field"**: Attribution and description confirmed. Simondon (1924–1989) developed the pre-individual/individuation framework; the chapter's usage is accurate.
- **Hugging Face publishing open model weights**: Factually accurate description of Hugging Face's function.

## Notes

- The chapter attributes the *body without organs* and deterritorialization concepts to Deleuze throughout, without mentioning Guattari. This is a common and defensible shorthand in popular philosophical writing, but Guattari scholars would consider it a significant omission. Jason should decide whether the book's intellectual commitments require acknowledging Guattari as co-author of these concepts — particularly given the book's emphasis on collective knowledge production over individual genius.
- The description of the Matrix's hollow-book scene is accurate in substance. One minor detail: the prop in the film is a blue hardcover mock-up rather than the familiar white paperback edition of *Simulacra and Simulation*, and the book is opened to the essay "On Nihilism" — these details are accurate to the scene but aren't relevant to the chapter's argument, so no action needed.
- All four internet-era dates (GPL 1989, web proposal 1989, Linux 1991, public domain 1993) check out. The chapter presents them as a four-year window; technically GPL (February 1989) to public domain release (April 1993) spans just over four years, which the text accurately characterizes as "four years."
