## Checked and Clear

- **Tim Berners-Lee / WWW / 1993**: CERN released the World Wide Web protocols to the public domain on April 30, 1993. Accurate.
- **Linus Torvalds / Linux / 1991**: Linux kernel first publicly released in September 1991. Accurate.
- **Richard Stallman / GPL / 1989**: GNU General Public License v1 released February 25, 1989. Accurate.
- **David Graeber as "anthropologist"**: Confirmed. Graeber was a professor of anthropology at LSE (and previously Yale and Goldsmiths).
- **Graeber's *Debt* and its argument**: The book is *Debt: The First 5,000 Years* (2011). The chapter references it simply as *Debt*, which is common shorthand. The summary of its argument (quantified obligation, gift economies, five thousand years of history) is accurate.
- **Graeber's concept of "baseline communism"**: Confirmed. Graeber developed this concept in *Debt* and related essays to describe the default communal ethic operating in any functioning community.
- **Hardt and Negri, *Declaration*, 2012**: Confirmed published 2012 as a self-published pamphlet/Kindle single in response to the 2011 movements. Accurate.
- **H&N subject-figures (indebted, mediatized, securitized, represented)**: These are the four subjective figures named in *Declaration*. Accurate.
- **Törbel community / 1483**: Confirmed. Törbel's commons governance has been documented to a treaty of 1483. This is the first case study in Elinor Ostrom's *Governing the Commons* (1990). Accurate.
- **Deleuze and Guattari / "desiring production" / desire as production not lack**: Confirmed. Central concept from *Anti-Oedipus* (1972). Accurate.
- **Jane Jacobs / "sidewalk ballet"**: Confirmed. From *The Death and Life of Great American Cities* (1961). Accurate.
- **Christopher Alexander / pattern language**: Reference is implicit ("Alexander's pattern language") — concept confirmed, from *A Pattern Language* (1977). Accurate.
- **Gramsci / "named the strategy"**: The chapter's use refers to his concept of the Modern Prince and hegemonic strategy — accurate as a broad attribution.
- **Simondon / pre-individual field**: Confirmed. Gilbert Simondon's philosophy of individuation centers on the pre-individual as a charged field from which singularities emerge. Accurate.
- **Cooperation Jackson / "building from the most marginalized"**: Confirmed. Cooperation Jackson is a network of worker cooperatives in Jackson, Mississippi, focused on Black and working-class communities. Accurate.
- **"Preguntando caminamos"**: Confirmed Zapatista saying, meaning "Asking, we walk." Accurate.
- **Federici / fifteenth century / commons enclosure pattern**: Federici's *Caliban and the Witch* (2004) documents enclosure of commons from the late 15th–17th centuries. The reference to "the same pattern Federici documented in the fifteenth century" is accurate in broad strokes. Accurate.
- **Arundhati Roy / Narmada valley**: Roy documented displacement and suppression of commons-based communities in the Narmada valley. Accurate.
- **Martín Prechtel / "Flowering Earth"**: Used as a conceptual reference, not a book title. Prechtel's work draws on Tzutujil Mayan traditions where the earth is understood as a flowering living being. The phrase is consistent with his teachings even if not a specific published book title. Acceptable as conceptual attribution.

## Notes

The chapter is factually clean. The three flags above are the only items warranting author attention before publication. The most substantive is the Zapatista caracoles / "thirty years" claim, which conflates the caracoles' lifespan with the broader movement's lifespan. The Sorenson attribution is a gap that should be addressed. The city count is minor and a judgment call.
