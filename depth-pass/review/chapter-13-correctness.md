## Notes

- The chapter contains one confirmed internal inconsistency: it describes the Debt Collective winning "$6 billion" in its introductory summary and correctly states "$5.8 billion" in the narrative section. The correction brings the opening paragraph into alignment with the body.
- The North End error (Irish vs. Italian) is the most significant factual error. The North End does have an Irish history — it was predominantly Irish in the 1840s–1880s — but by the time Jacobs wrote about it (and by the time it was redlined and thriving informally), it was Italian. The Irish history is real but misdirects the attribution.
- The Rolling Jubilee figures ($700,000 / $15 million) are in the right ballpark but may be slightly high compared to contemporaneous reporting. These are flagged rather than corrected because Jason may have access to the final published totals from the Rolling Jubilee fund, which were not uniformly reported.
