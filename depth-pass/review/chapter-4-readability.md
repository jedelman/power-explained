# Readability Pass: Chapter 4 — Nobody Asked Him To

## Changes Made

| Location | Original | Revised | Reason |
|----------|----------|---------|--------|
| Para 4 (Arizmendi students) | "...200 kilometers away — working fifty-five-hour weeks by day, studying at night." | "...200 kilometers away — they worked fifty-five-hour weeks by day and studied at night." | The original participial phrase ("working fifty-five-hour weeks by day, studying at night") attaches ambiguously to Arizmendi rather than to his students. A real reader could stumble, wondering whether it was Arizmendi or the students doing the 55-hour weeks. The subject of the preceding clause is Arizmendi ("he selected," "arranged for them"), so the dangling participle pulls the action back to him. Switching to an explicit subject ("they worked") removes the ambiguity without changing the sentence's rhythm or meaning. |

## Notes

This is one of the cleaner chapters in terms of readability. The prose is doing what it intends: building through accumulation, letting facts land without commentary, and ending with the author's own stake in the argument. The voice is consistent throughout.

The only genuine reader-stumble was the pronoun ambiguity in the Arizmendi section. Everything else is intentional style that should be left alone.

One observation for the author: the Jackson, Mississippi statistics ("median household income $33,000, poverty rate 34% among Black residents") were not checked in the correctness pass due to sequence. These would benefit from a quick fact-check against a dated source — unanchored poverty statistics become outdated quickly. This is a correctness note, not a readability note.

The personal section (Norfolk restaurant) reads as a controlled break in register — honest and unguarded in a way that is structurally earned after five case studies. The sentence "It cost me my idealism and my faith in humanity" is the emotional peak and the most vulnerable line in the chapter. What follows it earns it back without undoing it. That balance is working well.
