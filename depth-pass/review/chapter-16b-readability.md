## Notes

The prose is in good shape. The voice is controlled and consistent: direct, conceptually dense where it needs to be, short and punchy at the emotional beats. The chapter doesn't have the over-explanation problem noted in earlier chapters' style guidelines — it earns its abstractions by grounding them first.

Two patterns worth the author knowing about:

1. **"Named X as Y" construction**: Found once ("named this structure as"). Worth scanning other chapters for this — it's a common redundancy that can sneak into analytical prose.

2. **Relative pronoun omission before "that"-clauses**: The elided "that" in "the same thing indigenous navigators...have always known" is the clearest stumble in the chapter. This kind of omission is generally fine in speech but creates a brief parsing glitch in dense analytical prose. Worth staying alert to in revision.
