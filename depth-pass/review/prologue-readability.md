## Considered but Left

| Location | Element | Why Left |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Para. 2 (hospital bill), "and that's not really an answer, and you both know that, but it's the only answer available" | Triple-and chain followed by "but" | Creates a tangle on first read, but the run-on mimics the experience of being on hold — bureaucratic, airless. Changing it would flatten a deliberate tonal choice. Left. |
| Para. 3 ("Or it's the highway…") | Long compound sentence with three "Or" clauses | Could be broken up, but the accumulation is the point. Each clause carries the same weight; breaking them into separate sentences would create false punctuation in the argument. Left. |
| Para. 1 of Part I ("the one that took the houses of the people who couldn't fight back") | "the people who couldn't fight back" | Slightly wordy (could be "those who couldn't fight back"), but the full phrase has weight — "the people" rather than "those" keeps the subjects human. Left. |
| Part IV outline, "built and defended and continue to build and defend" | Doubled construction | Initially looks like a stumble, but it's deliberate anaphora — past action mirrored in present tense. Left. |
| "What kind of book" section, "maneuvering conditions so that a connection can happen" | Unusual phrasing | "Maneuvering conditions" is slightly unexpected but precisely right — it captures the facilitator's role (not teaching, not directing). A different reader might say "creating conditions," but that would be a style change, not a clarity fix. Left. |
| Part I outline, "starting with a crossing in Norfolk, Virginia, and the neighborhood built over to make it" | "it" refers back to the crossing | Minor pronoun distance, but the meaning is recoverable from context. Changing "it" to "the crossing" would insert an awkward repetition. Left. |
| Part III outline, "the construction of the human category itself as a mechanism for deciding who counts" | Dense noun phrase | Complex, but no real ambiguity. This is precise language for a precise idea. Left. |

## Notes

The prologue is in strong shape. The prose voice is consistent throughout — direct, grounded, emotionally controlled. The opening sequence (rent, hospital, highway, factory, app) earns its crescendo. The heron section is the strongest piece of writing in the chapter: the emotional logic is clear without being explained.

One structural observation for the author: the "About this book" section (Part IV) is tighter than the "What kind of book" section (Part V). The negative definitions ("not a manifesto," "not a policy book," "not an academic book") are effective, but "What it is: a guide" then runs for a full paragraph that slightly overcrowds the landing. This is not a readability fix — it's a structural question. The content is earned. Worth knowing.
