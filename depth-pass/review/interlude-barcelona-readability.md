# Readability Pass: Interlude: Barcelona, After the Fish Market

## Changes Made

| Location | Original | Revised | Reason |
|----------|----------|---------|--------|
| Para 4 (superblocks sentence) | "returning it to the people who live on the streets those cars cut through" | "returning it to the people who live on the streets that those cars cut through" | Omitting the relative pronoun "that" causes "streets those cars" to run together before the reader resolves the clause structure. A real reader stumbles briefly. Adding "that" fixes the parse without changing rhythm or meaning. |

## Considered but Left

| Location | Element | Why Left |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Para 2 | "No permit required, probably." | Unconventional comma and trailing qualifier, but clearly intentional — it enacts the narrator's amused uncertainty. Not a stumble; a style choice. |
| Para 5 | "exactly what you would expect people to do when given a street and an evening" | "Exactly what you would expect" slightly softens surprise, but removing it would alter the rhetorical register — earned modesty before the punchline "they made something together that none of them could have made alone." Style choice, not a clarity problem. |
| Para 7 | "The ease of knowing that life goes on even if yours doesn't." | "Yours" is loosely referential — the antecedent is "your life," implied. A reader pauses briefly, but the ambiguity appears deliberate and the meaning resolves cleanly in context. Fixing it risks flattening a line that earns its weight. |
| Para 8 | Long sentence ending "the climate that no one owns and everyone depends on" | Structurally complex, but each clause tracks without misreading. Length matches the scope of the claim — accumulation is the point. |
| Para 9 | Dash-enclosed clause "who decides what the street is for, who decides how the water is used, who bears the costs of the decisions and who captures the benefits" | Long interruption, but "is one question" clearly refers back to "The question of who governs shared resources." No ambiguity for a careful reader; the structure holds. |

## Notes

This is among the cleanest prose in the manuscript. Fragments ("Then salsa." / "Not a victory. Not an event. Just a Tuesday.") are clearly intentional and effective. The experiential-to-analytical pivot — "Barcelona has also been running out of water" — is handled well; "also" does real linking work without announcing the connection.

One change made: the missing relative pronoun in the superblocks sentence. Everything else is clean or deliberately unconventional.

Word count is approximately 450. The chapter is already tight; no further cuts are warranted.
