## Notes

This is a strong chapter with well-controlled voice and pacing. The prose moves at different tempos across sections — the Narmada opening is compressed and declarative; the Freire section has more clause-work; the personal anecdote opens up into longer sentences before tightening at the close. That variation is working.

One change was made (the repeated phrase in paras. 93 and 95). Everything else is intentional or borderline. The chapter does not have the explanatory scaffolding problem that earlier pieces had — it trusts the reader throughout.

The inline FLAG and CORRECTION markers added during the correctness pass do interrupt readability of the raw file, but they are working documents for human review, not permanent prose. They do not affect the final reading experience once resolved.

The most significant issue for the author's attention remains the Freire timeline flag from the correctness pass: the sentence "in the same decade Roy was learning to report, Paulo Freire was running adult literacy programs" implies contemporaneity that isn't there. If the sentence is meant as a structural rather than chronological parallel, a small signal — "On the other side of the world, in an earlier decade" or simply dropping "in the same decade" — would resolve it without altering the argument.
