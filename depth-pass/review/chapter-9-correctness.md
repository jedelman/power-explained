## Flags for Human Review

| Location | Claim | Uncertainty |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| Para 1, prison writing | "over the next six years" | The six-year figure is accurate for the active writing period (February 1929 – 1935), but the phrase "next six years" follows immediately after describing the 1928 trial, which could imply 1928–1934. The writing began three years after arrest, not immediately. Technically defensible but potentially misleading. |

Verify which date Jason intends as the movement's marker. If the intent is to mark the first urban/municipal model, 1984 is correct; if the first CLT generally, 1969 is correct. |

## Checked and Clear

- **Gramsci's birth and death**: Born January 22, 1891, in Ales, Sardinia; died April 27, 1937, age 46. Confirmed correct.
- **Gramsci's age at arrest**: Arrested November 9, 1926; age 35. Confirmed correct.
- **Turin factory occupations**: September 1920. Confirmed correct.
- **Mussolini's rise**: In power within two years of the 1920 occupations (October 1922). Confirmed correct.
- **Prosecutor quote**: "We must stop this brain from functioning for twenty years." Confirmed — this is the standard rendering; some sources render as "For twenty years we must stop this brain from functioning." The meaning is identical.
- **Prison Notebooks page count**: "nearly three thousand pages" — confirmed; sources consistently cite more than 3,000 pages.
- **Gramsci's imprisonment duration**: "eleven years of confinement." Arrested 1926, died 1937 — eleven years. Confirmed correct.
- **University of Turin scholarship**: 1911. Confirmed correct.
- **L'Ordine Nuovo**: Confirmed as the journal Gramsci edited. Correct.
- **Arizmendiarrieta and bicycle**: Sources confirm he traveled by bicycle as a matter of practice. "On his bike in the Basque mountains" is consistent with documented accounts.
- **Mondragon founding**: Five former students, 1956. Confirmed — the five founders of Ulgor (Luis Usatorre, Jesús Larrañaga, Alfonso Gorroñogoitia, José María Ormaechea, Javier Ortubay) founded the first cooperative in April 1956.
- **Arizmendiarrieta starting a school**: Confirmed — he founded the Escuela Politécnica Profesional in Mondragón.
- **Valencia Water Tribunal**: "a thousand years of common sense." Sources confirm roots in the 10th century (Islamic period) — approximately a thousand years. Confirmed.
- **Zapatista uprising**: 1994. Confirmed correct.
- **Zapatista autonomous communities**: Governance structures, schools in indigenous languages, health clinics, cooperative enterprises. Confirmed consistent with documented accounts.
- **Interregnum quote**: "The crisis consists precisely in the fact that the old is dying and the new cannot be born; in this interregnum a great variety of morbid symptoms appear." Confirmed accurate rendering.
- **Gramsci's father's profession**: "minor civil servant." Confirmed — Francesco Gramsci was a low-level administrative official.
- **Gramsci's childhood work**: "land registry office." Confirmed — sources state he worked in a Land Registry (Catasto) office.
- **"fourth of seven children"**: Confirmed — fourth of seven sons (some sources say "seven children," others "seven sons"). No discrepancy that affects the claim.

## Notes

- The chapter refers to "Arizmendi" throughout without the full name "Arizmendiarrieta." If this figure appears in Part II with the full name, the shorthand is fine; if not, readers may not have the referent. Not a factual error — a structural question for the author.
- The claim that the interregnum quote "has stayed with readers for ninety years" is approximately correct (notebooks written ~1929-1935, so 90-95+ years depending on when the chapter was written). Acceptable approximation.
- "He was writing about Italian fascism" — scholarly debate exists about what exactly Gramsci meant in the interregnum passage (some argue he was referring to the Italian Communist Party's ultraleft turn, not fascism directly). The text's claim is the mainstream popular reading and is not incorrect, but it slightly simplifies a contested interpretive question.
