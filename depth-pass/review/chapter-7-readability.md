# Readability Pass: Chapter 7 — "He Wanted His Nerves Back"

## Changes Made

| Location | Original | Revised | Reason |
|----------|----------|---------|--------|
| Final section, opening sentence | "Together they reach something the last two chapters' economic analysis couldn't fully close." | "Together Fanon and Mbembe reach something the last two chapters' economic analysis couldn't fully close." | After the long neighborhood narrative ending with "nothing we can do," the pronoun "they" has no grammatically proximate antecedent — the most recent subjects are "they" (city officials) and the chapter-level subjects Fanon and Mbembe have not appeared in several paragraphs. A real reader may stumble on who "they" refers to. Naming them resolves the ambiguity cleanly without changing rhythm or removing content. |

## Notes

The prose is in strong shape. The voice is consistent throughout — direct, accumulating, grounded in specifics. The emotional arc works: Richmond opening, Fanon's clinic, the structure visible from inside, Mbembe's plane window, the meeting room, the commons refusing the classification.

The one genuine readability issue was the pronoun "they" in the final section opener, now resolved.

The long sentence in the *Black Skin, White Masks* paragraph was considered for breaking but left intact: it earns its length by performing exactly the impossible recursive project it describes (the sentence about proving something wrong to people whose recognition you can't stop wanting is itself an example of the endless qualification that project produces).

The "alibi / mirror" section's short declarative sentences are a deliberate rhetorical pattern and were not touched.

The correction and flag markers added during the correctness pass are clearly marked and will not affect readability of the final text once Jason resolves them.
