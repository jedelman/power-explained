## Notes

This is exceptionally clean prose for a first chapter. The voice is consistent and controlled throughout — short declarative sentences used for impact, long sentences for immersion and accumulation, fragments as deliberate beats. The structural rhythm (short → accumulation → short) is working.

The one mechanical fix (comma in the "narrow enough" clause) is the only place a real reader stumbles on a grammatical boundary. Everything else that might look like roughness is intentional shaping.

The correctness-pass FLAG and CORRECTION markers are inline and will interrupt a reader's experience of the text — that's by design for the review process. Jason should resolve both before publication.

The truck traffic sentence (line 17) is worth Jason's eye: the compression of "truck traffic that containers would have moved by ferry" is semantically unusual and may need a light rewording if it reads oddly to other readers. It's a small thing in an otherwise strong passage.
