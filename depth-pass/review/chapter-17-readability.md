## Notes

This chapter is exceptionally well-composed. The prose sustains a consistent meditative register — direct, specific, building argument through image rather than assertion — with very few places where a reader stumbles on mechanics rather than ideas.

The one real readability fix (word order of "only") is minor. The chapter's primary risks are in factual territory (addressed in the correctness pass) rather than prose construction.

The section on Jane Jacobs and Alexander is where the chapter is most at risk of slipping into abstraction. The Alexander section (especially the patent sentence) is harder to track than the Jacobs section. If revision is made based on the Moses/expressway flag in the correctness pass, the author may find a natural opportunity to tighten the transition into Alexander.

No word count reduction was applied. The prose in this chapter does not carry the redundancy that warranted 25-35% cuts elsewhere. The existing length is earned.
