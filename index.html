01
The hardest thing to understand

Nobody is running this. That's the point.

When things go badly — when the rent is too high, when the news makes you anxious, when your job feels meaningless — it's natural to look for who's responsible.

There are responsible people. CEOs make real choices. Politicians pick sides. Platforms decide what you see. Those choices matter, and we should name them.

But here's what most explanations miss: if you replaced every one of them tomorrow, most of the same things would keep happening. Not because the new people are secretly the same. Because the rules of the game produce the outcomes, not just the players.

The machine runs itself. Understanding that is the first step to doing anything real about it.

02
What most people miss

The whole system needs you. You don't need it.

Here's something they'd rather you not sit with too long.

Every platform, every app, every media company — they run on something they can't make themselves. Your attention. Your creativity. Your relationships. Your knowledge of your own neighborhood, your own life, your own people.

They can harvest it. They can package it and sell it. But they can't produce it. When you share knowledge with a neighbor, when you help someone through a hard time, when people build something together — that's real. That's the raw material.

The thing they're selling started with you. That's not a small thing.

03
Two kinds of systems

Some things capture. Some things build.

There's a useful way to look at almost anything: ask whether it's designed to capture value or to build it.

A platform that takes your attention and sells it to advertisers is capturing. A community that pools resources to help members in a crisis is building. An algorithm that keeps you angry and clicking is capturing. A neighborhood tool library where people share what they have is building.

Most of what we interact with every day is designed to capture. Most of what actually keeps people alive — fed, housed, not alone — is built through cooperation.

You already know the difference. You've felt it. This is just giving it a name.
