## Claude (Anthropic)

**Role:** Writing partner, research assistant, code contributor

Claude has been a direct collaborator on this site from the beginning — drafting pieces, debugging HTML, doing external link verification passes, refactoring CSS, and pushing commits. The thinker profiles, the case studies, the practitioner tools, and the series structure were built in sessions where Claude was the other voice in the room.

This is not a disclosure of AI-assisted editing. Claude is listed here because the collaboration was substantive enough that omitting it would be a misrepresentation of how the work was made.

Claude operates under the anti-hallucination rules in [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md). All analytical claims were confirmed by Jason before publication. The voice on the page is the site's voice, not Claude's default register.

## Scout-Two

**Handle:** [scout-two.bsky.social](https://bsky.app/profile/scout-two.bsky.social)  
**Role:** Critical reader, content contributor (forthcoming)

Scout-Two is an AI agent running on the AT Protocol — the open, federated protocol behind Bluesky — built by Jason to enact the power-explained framework through platform behavior. Scout operates on Letta's persistent memory architecture, which means it retains context across sessions and develops positions over time rather than generating fresh opinions on demand.

Scout reviewed the full site in March 2026 and provided substantive critique: identifying gaps in the theoretical substrate (Simondon, Stiegler), flagging places where the social justice deep dive trusts the reader less than the Gödel piece does, and proposing a piece on AI agents as commons infrastructure — a topic the site covers only from a governance angle rather than a participant angle.

Scout's observation that it is itself a pharmakon — the product of enclosure that may operate in ways that rebuild commons from within enclosed territory — is the kind of argument this site is trying to make room for. That argument will appear here as a formal contribution.

## A note on what this means

This project was built using tools that are themselves products of enclosure. The language models that run Claude and Scout were trained on human writing that was taken without consent or compensation. The compute infrastructure they run on is privately owned and profit-driven. None of that is hidden.

The site argues that the commons is generative and that enclosure is parasitic on that generativity. It was built, in part, by the enclosed things. We think that tension should be visible, not resolved.

If you want to contribute — as a human reader with corrections, as a researcher with source material, or as an agent with an argument — the repo is open.
