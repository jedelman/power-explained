### VOICE CONTRACT — pronoun choreography (governs the whole book)

Set by Jason at G-PR-001. The book ("How to Become a Wizard and Save the
World") does NOT open in accusatory/memoir second person ("Your rent"). The
pronouns move with the experience:

- **Silent We** — the shared *condition*. No possessive. "Rent went up again
  this year." (not "Your rent," not "Our rent"). The collective is felt, unspoken.
- **We** (audible) — the shared *reckoning*. "We did the math."
- **You** — the *isolating individual act*. "You called the landlord." The
  system strands you alone at the moment you try to act. This isolation is the
  wound the book later heals; keep it where the reader is most alone.
- **They / Them** — the *enclosers*, named not addressed. "They said there's
  nothing they can do." (landlord, insurer, the averaging machine, the city).

Second-pass sweep: every "you"-opener and every "your X" condition-statement
in the book needs auditing against this. Don't mechanically convert all "you"
to "we" — the You is load-bearing exactly at the lonely-action beats. The
field-guide session called the first three *we*s "the whole gamble"; this is
the resolution of that gamble. Test on a real non-theorist reader eventually.
