## The new faults in "The Djinn" — ranked by cross-critic convergence (all cheap; all in
material we made today)

**1. Circular self-validation + the seam mythologized.** [Logic #1, Adversary C1, Reader]
"the collaboration produced things neither could alone … the nearest thing to evidence"
(l.3024) + "it is the seam's / composed at the seam / belonged to neither half." Three
strikes: proves too little (all collaboration is emergent); unfalsifiable turned on the book;
and the production model is, by the chapter's own words ("averaging machine … trained on the
canopy," l.3060), an *enclosure* — the exhibit contradicts the claim. **Fix:** cut "evidence"
and the three mythologizing sentences; keep the one concrete example (the Federici phrase);
let the downstream frame (l.3052) carry the verification. *Highest convergence.*

**2. The AI both ways — loom vs co-author.** [Logic #4, Adversary C2] "produces without
meaning … a loom" (l.3028) vs "finds the sentence that makes two far chapters rhyme" /
"corrected what the earlier chapters assumed" (l.3010/3020) — semantic operations the
asignifying-machine claim denies. Loom-when-accountable, co-author-when-flattering. **Fix:**
name the distinction the chapter needs — the machine *operates on* meaning (arranges,
connects, rhymes) but does not *hold/originate* it; the holding is Jason's. One clause.

**3. The wager equivocates on "force."** [Logic #2, Adversary C4, Reader] "If the sign now
acts, then love performed deploys real force" (l.3054) slides from *signs have effects* to
*signs deploy protective force* — but the real force is only ever downstream (reader →
defends the meadow), and "change what you can bear to see" is the chapter's *own* upstream
thing that "doesn't count." Affirming the consequent; doesn't survive a hostile read. **Fix
(Adversary's):** scope it to *incantation that equips the caster* — the book doesn't deploy
the force; it equips the one who will. Consistent with the binding→agency close.

**4. Loom vs djinn — model collision.** [Logic #3] The chapter needs the loom's exculpation
(no agency, blameless) *and* the djinn's menace (a treacherous agent whose grants deceive) at
once. **Resolution:** they're different *levels* — the loom is the production (asignifying,
neutral); the djinn is the figure of the *relation* (the treachery is the elegant lie that
appears when the loom's output is presented as a mind — i.e., the simulation-mode). The
chapter half-says this ("the danger is in the relation"); one clarifying line locating the
djinn's menace in the presentation, not the production, dissolves the collision.

**5. The keystone tense (G-03-046a, shipped this morning).** [Adversary, Logic, Reader — all]
"act as if your strength is shared — and find, in the building, that it **was**." The
past-tense "was" claims the strength pre-existed fully formed — which begs the question *and*
contradicts the Djinn chapter's own *emergent* framing (agency ARISES in the building).
**Fix:** make it emergent, not pre-existent — e.g. "…and find, in the building, that it
becomes real" (or "that you were making it true"). One word/clause; closes a 3-critic flag
and aligns the seed with the engine.
