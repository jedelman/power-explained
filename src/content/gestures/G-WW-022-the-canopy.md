The architecture is different in a hostile environment. You do not get to choose the ideal site; you choose what cover you can raise on the site you are on, and the architecture starts as that cover. The restaurant was a canopy. We managed the downpour instead of holding out for a better day. And once you are running a canopy, certain things you would have built on a dry site you do not get to build. We ended up not opening a tea room for the sober-curious socialites. We ended up not opening a nature school. Those things would have wanted their own canopies in their own weather, and the weather was already taking everything we had. The structures you do not build are part of the architecture too. They are the shape of what the canopy was costing.
