Roy didn't stumble into the Narmada valley and spontaneously see what the dam project's accounting couldn't. She had trained herself to attend — to go to the specific place, talk to the specific person, follow the specific document trail, sit with what she found rather than processing it immediately into a position. The witness is not passive. It is an extremely active practice of refusing to look away, refusing to aggregate, refusing to let the accounting substitute for the presence of the person being accounted for. At its furthest extension, the witness doesn't only observe the place — the witness becomes it, takes on its conditions, learns to see from inside the relationship rather than outside it.
