The implication for commons-building is direct and uncomfortable. A commons built from the most advantaged among its dependents will reproduce the exclusions it was supposed to overcome. It will look inclusive while functionally serving those whose power makes them most legible. Genuine commons governance must start from the most excluded: must ask, at every stage, whose experience the current framework cannot see, and build toward that rather than away from it. That is slower. It is the only way to build something that actually threatens enclosure rather than constructing a smaller version of it.
