The night herons know about the road in ways we are still learning to understand — their territory maps include the crossing, the people who bless the nests are part of what they know about this place. The doves know about the argument in their way: they feel the quieted body that years of practice produced. The deer that walked through the camp in Washington knew something too — and the twenty five-year-olds who dropped into the stalking gait were learning to know it with them. Life is what we have in common — the herons and the doves and the deer and the people and the elders and the sugarcane worker writing his name and the employee at the bar, telling the story proudly, not needing me to be at the center of it.
