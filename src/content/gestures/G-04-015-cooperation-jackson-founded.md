Cooperation Jackson was founded there in 2014, rooted in decades of Black liberation organizing and in the Jackson-Kush Plan developed by organizers in the Malcolm X Grassroots Movement. It was connected to the election of Chokwe Lumumba as mayor in June 2013 — Lumumba won the Democratic primary with 86 percent of the vote despite being massively outspent. He had been a leader of the Republic of New Afrika, a Mississippi human rights attorney for decades, and a member of the Jackson City Council.
