Why did the children need to be brought there at all? Because the world they had been born into had cast a small spell on them: the assumption that the important things happen indoors, on screens, between adults, far from anything alive. The book calls it *common sense* — the slow incantation by which one view of the world becomes the background everyone breathes. Incantation is not the tool of the powerful. It is what produces them, by producing the subjects who consent. The educator's craft is the counter-spell: not louder, not more insistent, but a different incantation. *Take them there.* Maneuver the conditions. Trust the body to remember what the spell trained it to forget. The deer is the proof: the body had not forgotten.
