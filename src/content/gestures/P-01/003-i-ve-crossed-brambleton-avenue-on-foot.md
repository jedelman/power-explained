I've crossed Brambleton Avenue on foot hundreds of times. I've walked cities on multiple continents — Barcelona, London, Seattle, New York — and I know what it feels like to be a pedestrian in a city designed around you, that assumes your existence and accounts for it. Norfolk is not that city.
