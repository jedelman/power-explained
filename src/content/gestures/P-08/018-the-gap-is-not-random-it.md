The gap is not random. It systematically excludes the most vulnerable — the people whose oppression is produced by the combination of categories that each single-axis framework addresses separately. The labor movement that organized white workers and excluded Black workers. The feminist movement that fought for white women's suffrage while forming alliances with segregationists. The disability rights movement built around white, middle-class disabled people. In each case, the framework was built from the most advantaged among its intended constituency. The people at the intersections fell through — and the pattern of who fell through was not coincidental. It reflected whose experience had shaped the framework's construction.
