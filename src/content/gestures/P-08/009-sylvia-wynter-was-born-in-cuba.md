Sylvia Wynter was born in Cuba in 1928 and grew up in Jamaica. She studied in London on a scholarship, became a novelist and playwright, spent years trying to write the Caribbean into literary existence — not as backdrop for European stories but as the center of its own history, its own ways of being human. She was doing this in the 1950s and 1960s, when the canon was entirely European, when to write from the Caribbean was to write from somewhere the dominant culture classified as peripheral.
