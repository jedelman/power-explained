Angela Davis has been building in exactly that direction longer than most people have been thinking about it.

She was born in Birmingham, Alabama in 1944, in the neighborhood called Dynamite Hill — named for how often the Ku Klux Klan bombed the homes of Black families who dared to move there. She went to Brandeis on a scholarship, studied in Frankfurt with Adorno and Marcuse, came back to teach at UCLA, was fired by Ronald Reagan's Board of Regents for her political associations, was charged with murder on fabricated evidence, was acquitted after an international campaign. She was twenty-eight. [CORRECTION: "twenty-seven" -> "twenty-eight" | Davis was born January 26, 1944, and was acquitted June 4, 1972 -- she had turned 28 in January 1972.] She has been building ever since.
