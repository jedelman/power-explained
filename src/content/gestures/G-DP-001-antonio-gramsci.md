**Antonio Gramsci** — *hegemony · the war of position*

He grew up in Ales, Sardinia, the fourth of seven children of a minor civil servant who was imprisoned for five years on embezzlement charges when Gramsci was seven — leaving the family in poverty, leaving Gramsci to work in a land registry office as a child to help support them. He was small, hunched from a childhood spinal condition, brilliant. He won a scholarship to the University of Turin in 1911, arriving in the city of Fiat — steel, industrial noise, a working class nothing like the Sardinian peasants he had grown up among — and discovered Marx and the industrial proletariat in the same semester. By 1919 he was at the center of Turin's factory-council movement, editing *L'Ordine Nuovo*. Mussolini's regime imprisoned him; he died in 1937, six days after his conditional release, his body destroyed by eleven years of confinement. He was forty-six. The prison notebooks were smuggled out in sections.
