The chapter has stayed in one place: the belly, the diaphragm, the breath the body forgets to finish. Reich named what happens there when the holding has gone on too long — the armor, the history pressed into muscle. The floor is where the holding gave out, and where the belly opened, which is not the same thing. The woods are what it had been bracing against: other people, the fire, the ground. The tradition gave the belly something to do when it had a choice. The shrug is the weight pressed in from outside before anyone asked. The descent is where a body goes when the belly has been closed long enough to forget it can open. The belly and the breath. Now you have an address.
