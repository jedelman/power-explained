Robinson and Rodney named the structure. Fanon and Mbembe named what the structure does to the person inside it. Two motions of the same demon.

*Incantation* casts subjectivity on the mind — the slow speech that turns *the way things are* into *the way things must be*. Race as the categorization that explains the dispossession. *Nothing we can do*, said until it sounds like a fact. *Inscription* does to the body what incantation does to the mind — cuts the structure into the flesh, the nervous system, the gait, the gaze, the ambient knowing of which spaces are yours and which ones require you to manage something. The yanked arm. The shrug taught early. The shoulders that drop where the body has been told it can rest, and tighten where it has been told it cannot.

Paulo Freire, writing *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* in exile from Brazil in 1968, held the harder part: *both* sides of the line are inscribed. The colonized's body is marked at the sites of harm; the mind is taught the explanation that makes the harm look like nature. The colonizer's body is marked at the sites of ease; the mind is taught the explanation that makes the ease look like merit. Different inscriptions. The pathology Fanon treated walked into his clinic from both sides of the line for a reason.

This is why removing the colonizer is not yet liberation. The colony in the mind. The colony in the bone. The flag comes down; the inscription continues, the incantation continues. The work is to break both.
