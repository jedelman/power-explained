On the other side of the world, decades earlier, Paulo Freire had run adult literacy programs in the northeast of Brazil — one of the poorest regions in the world, where the plantation economy had left generations of agricultural workers landless, illiterate, and legally excluded from political participation. In Brazil at the time, you had to be literate to vote. This was not a coincidence.

The standard literacy curriculum presented words as technical objects to be memorized. Freire tried something different: he started with what he called generative themes — the words and situations already alive in his students' experience. *Favela.* *Land.* *Vote.* *Work.* Not as vocabulary exercises but as situations to be investigated.
