On the other side of the world, in the same decade Roy was learning to report, Paulo Freire was running adult literacy programs in the northeast of Brazil [FLAG: timeline | Freire's Brazilian adult literacy programs ran from the late 1950s to 1964, when the military coup exiled him. Roy's Narmada reporting was the late 1990s — a different decade by 30+ years. If the framing is a structural parallel rather than a temporal claim, the sentence needs a clearer signal; as written it implies contemporaneity.] — one of the poorest regions in the world, where the plantation economy had left generations of agricultural workers landless, illiterate, and legally excluded from political participation. In Brazil at the time, you had to be literate to vote. This was not a coincidence.

The standard literacy curriculum presented words as technical objects to be memorized. Freire tried something different: he started with what he called generative themes — the words and situations already alive in his students' experience. *Favela.* *Land.* *Vote.* *Work.* Not as vocabulary exercises but as situations to be investigated.
