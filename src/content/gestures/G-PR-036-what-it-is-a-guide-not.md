What it is: a guide. Not a guide to policy or to action, exactly — but a guide to the kind of preparation that makes seeing possible. The seeing that makes action possible. The practice that, once it takes root, keeps growing even when you stop tending it deliberately. The nature educators who take children into old-growth forest aren't teaching ecology. They're tending conditions so that a connection can happen — so that the child can feel, understand, and remember what old means, what belonging to something larger means, what there is to protect. You cannot tell someone that. You can only put them in contact with it, and trust.

This book puts you in contact with the pattern — the structure of enclosure and the structure of the commons, near the people who built and defended and continue to build and defend — so that you can feel it rather than just know it. Questions of politics are always questions of consciousness. These pages are an invitation to shift, if you can take it.
