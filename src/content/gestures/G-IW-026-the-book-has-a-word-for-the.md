The book has a word for the educator and her colleagues, and for the children too. It is the same word that has been on the cover this whole time. It is *wizard*. Not a robe. Not a tower. Not a knowledge held above the room like a lamp no one may carry. A wizard is someone who has walked the terrain long enough to recognize it when it rises again — to know, at the edge of a clearing, that what is happening must not be interrupted. There is no point at which one has fully *become* a wizard. There is only the becoming. The educator was not a wizard. She was becoming one. Twenty years of practice, and she was still in it. The five-year-old who, sixty months into being alive, dropped into a stalking gait without being told — that child was also becoming one. They were at different points in the same becoming. There is no other kind.
