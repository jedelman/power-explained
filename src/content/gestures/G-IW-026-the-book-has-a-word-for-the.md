When you learn to read the terrain, to see the spells being cast, to cast your own and watch the magic work — what else can be said of you but that you are becoming a *wizard*? Someone who has walked the terrain long enough to recognize it when it rises again — to know, at the edge of a clearing, that what is happening must not be interrupted. There is no point at which one has fully *become* a wizard. There is only the becoming — the walking, the noticing, the reaching to be useful, ongoing. The educator was not a wizard. She was becoming one. Twenty years of practice, and she was still in it. The five-year-old who, sixty months into being alive, dropped into a stalking gait when the deer appeared — that child was also becoming one. They were at different points in the same becoming.
