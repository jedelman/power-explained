**Kimberlé Crenshaw** — *intersectionality*

She grew up in Canton, Ohio, went to Cornell, then Harvard Law School, where she was one of very few Black women in a profession built by and for a very specific kind of person. She arrived at the intersection constantly — in her coursework, in the cases she studied, in the frameworks she was being trained to use. The law could see race. It could see sex. It consistently could not see her — not because she was invisible, but because the categories it used had been built around other people's experiences, and the combination was unspeakable within the available frameworks. Reading *DeGraffenreid v. General Motors* thirteen years on, she named the gap.
