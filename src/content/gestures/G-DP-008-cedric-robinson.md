**Cedric Robinson** — *racial capitalism · the marooned*

He grew up in Oakland in the 1940s and arrived at Berkeley in the late 1950s, as the civil rights movement was forcing the question of who counted as a full subject of democratic life — a question being answered in the streets and in the bodies of people beaten on bridges in Alabama. He watched, and asked the one his contemporaries weren't asking: not *what specific injustice is this?* but *why does the pattern keep recurring?* The answer took twenty years to develop. It landed in 1983 as *Black Marxism: The Making of the Black Radical Tradition*. For most of the first decade after publication, it was out of print. The argument survived the indifference, because the condition it describes is still operating — and the people living inside it have not stopped thinking clearly about it.
