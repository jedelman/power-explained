What this costs: people with medical debt experience housing instability at significantly higher rates than those without — missed mortgage payments, eviction, a move to cheaper housing, homelessness. A 2024 study in JAMA Network Open found that a one percentage point increase in the share of a county's population carrying medical debt is associated with an extra year of life lost per thousand people, and elevated mortality rates across every leading cause of death including suicide. People in problem debt are three times more likely to consider suicide than those without. A significant share of U.S. suicides occur in response to financial crisis.
