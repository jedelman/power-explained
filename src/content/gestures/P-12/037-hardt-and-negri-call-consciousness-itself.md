Hardt and Negri call consciousness itself a commons — the accumulated shared capacity to perceive, interpret, and act on the world. Like any commons, it must be built, nourished, and defended. The enclosure of consciousness is not metaphorical: it happens through the curriculum that teaches which questions are answerable, through the media environment that substitutes outrage for attention, through the pace of work that leaves no time for the quality of presence that witnessing requires. The coup that imprisoned Freire understood this precisely. You don't need to suppress the conclusion if you can enclose the attention that would produce it.
