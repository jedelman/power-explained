Half a million people were displaced by the Narmada Valley Development Project. [FLAG: displacement figure | Sources vary significantly: Sardar Sarovar Dam alone is commonly cited at 250,000–320,000 displaced; the full Narmada Valley Development Project is cited at approximately 1.5 million. "Half a million" does not clearly match either figure. Verify against Roy's essay "The Greater Common Good" (1999).] The ones who received the promised relocation assistance were a minority of a minority. Many were relocated to land that was unusable — eroded, waterless, without the infrastructure that had been promised. Many were simply left.
