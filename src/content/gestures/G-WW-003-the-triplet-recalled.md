The angel pays attention. The council holds the seeing. The wizard looks for ways to be helpful. The book has built the first two carefully — the posture of facing backward into the wreckage, the perceptual commons that makes the seeing bearable. This chapter is about the third. The wizard's job begins when the seeing has been honored and the council has been gathered, and the question becomes: now what. The honest answer is almost never grand. The honest answer is usually small, specific, and addressed to the actual room you are in.
