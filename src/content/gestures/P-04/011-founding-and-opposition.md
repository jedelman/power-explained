In 1984, the Burlington Community Land Trust was founded with a $200,000 seed grant from the city, supplemented by a loan from the municipal pension fund matched by Burlington Savings Bank and federal HUD funds. It was the first community land trust in the United States to be initiated by a municipal government.

It was opposed. A group backed by real estate interests, Homeowners Against the Land Trust, picketed City Hall. Sanders and other progressive officials were red-baited — accused of seizing land and betraying property rights. The pushback was loudest in the Old North End, the working-class neighborhood where the trust was concentrating its early purchases. Some incumbent homeowners worried about property values. The Trust went ahead anyway.
