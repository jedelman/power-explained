What she did with it was go back to the ground. The Narmada. The forests. The nuclear tests India and Pakistan conducted in 1998, which she wrote about with the exact fury of someone who understood that both governments had used their poorest citizens as the justification for weapons that would never protect them. She stood somewhere, named what she found, and said whose side she was on. Her essays are not balanced presentations. They are acts of witness, which requires a position.
