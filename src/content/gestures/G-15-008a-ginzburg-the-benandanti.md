Federici names what the hunts removed. Carlo Ginzburg shows what was inside the thing being removed. In the Friuli, in the same centuries, there were the *benandanti* — "good walkers," who believed they left their bodies on certain nights to do battle, in spirit, for the harvest. They understood themselves not as witches but as the village's protectors against witchcraft. Ginzburg reconstructed them from the only records that survived: the transcripts of the Inquisition that came to destroy them. And he caught, on the page, what the trials actually did — decade by decade, question by question, the harvest-protector was reinterpreted into the very thing she fought. Not superstition cured by reason. A living body of folk knowledge, overwritten by an institution with the power to rename it. The witch was the enclosed knower.
