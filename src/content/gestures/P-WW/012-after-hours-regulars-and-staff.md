The doors had been locked behind the last guest. The lights had been turned down to the after-hours level — bright enough to count, dim enough to talk. A few regulars were still at the bar, the way regulars stay after hours when the room belongs to them as much as to anyone. The staff were drifting through their last tasks, glasses and silver, the slow re-setting of the floor. I was behind the bar, polishing glasses. There was a conversation going.
