One of my staff broke into the conversation. It was not their conversation. They had not been asked. They said something close to: "we don't serve natural wine just because it tastes good. It has to taste good *and* be good for everyone involved." Not as a correction. As a clarification — the way someone explains the rule of the room to a guest who has gotten part of it right and is missing the rest. No glance at me. No checking. They were not on the clock; nobody was rating them. They said the sentence the way you say something you have already thought all the way through, and went on with whatever they had been doing before they said it.
