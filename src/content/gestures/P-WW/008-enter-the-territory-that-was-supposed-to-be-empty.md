The fifth capacity is entering — actually going into the parts of the world the official map said were empty, dead, mute, or already settled. The meadow that was waiting to be a parking lot. The lineage the disenchantment project was supposed to have buried. The conversation with the herons in your actual voice. The neighbor whose porch you walked past for two years before you knocked. The territory was not empty; you were trained to read it as empty. The wizard enters anyway, gently, the way a guest enters a kitchen they have not been in before, ready to be welcomed or refused and not pretending to know which it will be.
