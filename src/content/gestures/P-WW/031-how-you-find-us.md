You will find us the same way we found each other. By becoming the kind of person who recognizes the room when you walk into it. By saying the second sentence at the bar without being asked. By being the one who shows up to the meadow with a notebook because someone has to start writing down what is growing there. By naming, in the kitchen of someone you barely know, the thing the kitchen has been quietly trying to say to you both. You find us by becoming someone we recognize on sight, which is the same as saying you find us by becoming the next us. There is a small cost to this. The door opens for you, and behind you it quietly stops opening in the other direction. You will not be able to go back to the version of yourself that the book first met. You will, instead, be the version of yourself the next reader is going to need to find.
