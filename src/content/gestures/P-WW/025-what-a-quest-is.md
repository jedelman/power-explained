A quest of this kind has three properties. It is local — it happens in a place you can name, that you actually go to, not in a category of places. It is bodily — there is something you have to do, with your hands or your voice or your time, that no theory can do for you. And it is specific — it is one thing, not a campaign of things. One meadow. One kid. One neighborhood. One reading group. One song. One conversation you have been avoiding for two years. The quest is not the cause. The cause is large, distributed, partial, and somebody else's job too. The quest is the piece of the cause that has your name on it. It is the piece that, if you do not show up for it, no one else will, because no one else is standing where you are standing.
