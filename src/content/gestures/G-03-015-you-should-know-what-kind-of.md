Its author, Garrett Hardin, was a director of the American Eugenics Society and a white nationalist. He co-founded anti-immigrant organizations and sat on the boards of population control groups. He would go on to write "Lifeboat Ethics: The Case Against Helping the Poor," arguing that the correct response to famine in the Global South is to just let people die.
