The book reshaped how primitive accumulation is read. Before Federici, the body had been theoretical missing matter. After her, it was where you started.

Academic Marxism resisted her for decades. The wages-for-housework movement she had co-founded in 1972 with Mariarosa Dalla Costa, Selma James, and Brigitte Galtier was dismissed as utopian — the wage demand misunderstood as policy proposal, when its point was to cost the labor in order to make it visible. She held the argument anyway. *Caliban* took twenty years to write. The witch was vindicated in the citation count.

And before all that — Nigeria. She lived and taught at the University of Port Harcourt through the 1980s. She watched the IMF's structural adjustment programs come for African universities, African markets, African commons. Luxemburg's mechanism, not as history but as Tuesday morning. She wrote about that too.

Her recent work names the counter-spell: *Re-Enchanting the World: Feminism and the Politics of the Commons*, 2019. The witch is back. So are the commons. The two were never separate.
