The Tzutujil refused corrugated metal roofing when it became available — not out of conservatism but from a completely lucid understanding of what they would lose. The traditional thatched roof needed to be repaired every year. The community gathered to do it. Neighbors came. Some thatched, some cooked, some fed children, some argued about the right way. Nobody called it community building. It was roofing. But through the roofing, the web of relationship was rewoven. The obligations were renewed. The community updated its knowledge of itself — who was strong this year, who needed help, who had learned something new. That is what the refusal protected.
