Freire's method was the same practice taught as a curriculum. Not: here is the correct analysis of your situation. But: look at this word you already know. What does it contain? What does it touch? Stay with it. The conscientization wasn't installed — it was what happened when people were given the conditions to attend to their own experience without the curriculum that had been busy teaching them not to.

Hardt and Negri call consciousness itself a commons — the accumulated shared capacity to perceive, interpret, and act on the world. Like any commons, it must be built, nourished, and defended. The enclosure of consciousness is not metaphorical: it happens through the curriculum that teaches which questions are answerable, through the media environment that substitutes outrage for attention, through the pace of work that leaves no time for the quality of presence that witnessing requires. The coup that imprisoned Freire understood this precisely. You don't need to suppress the conclusion if you can enclose the attention that would produce it.

This is the enclosure underneath all the other enclosures. The one that makes the rest possible. When the capacity to perceive clearly is itself enclosed — through curriculum, through pace, through the steady replacement of attention with noise — every other enclosure becomes easier to administer and harder to contest. The commons of consciousness is the foundation. It is also what the system most needs to take.

What becomes possible when enough of us cultivate that attention — not just hold the right positions, but actually see? Not a revolution in the sense of a single rupture — something harder to suppress, because it doesn't live in a single organization that can be dissolved or a single leader who can be imprisoned or killed. It lives in the accumulated capacity of people who have been present to what is actually happening, who have built or defended or joined something that operates on different assumptions, who have attended carefully enough to know the difference between the simulation and the thing. Roy standing in the water. Freire's student with the pen. The children moving through the forest because we had spent a week preparing them to notice.
