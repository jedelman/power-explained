Two years ago, the parks department mowed what had become a managed meadow — following a management plan, they said. I gave them hell. Their plan was based on appearances, not ecology. They said I was correct — their plan was calibrated to mitigate complaints from neighbors. They haven't changed the plan officially, but they haven't cut the meadow since. Every morning I walk past it expecting it to be cut. Every morning it isn't is a small triumph. I've seen bunnies there. One cold winter storm I watched a tiny kinglet — one of the smallest birds in North America — taking shelter in the tall grass, exactly where a meadow should be.
