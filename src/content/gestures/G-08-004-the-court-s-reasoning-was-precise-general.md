The court's reasoning was precise: General Motors wasn't discriminating against Black people — some Black men worked there. And it wasn't discriminating against women — some white women worked there. Therefore there was no discrimination. The Black women fell through the gap between the legally recognized categories. What was being done to them had no name the law could see.
