See the edges of the spell, too. The commons has not replaced the market — not yet. The two want different things. The market coordinates the wants of millions of strangers who will never meet; the commons holds the things that die the moment they are sold. Whether a commons could coordinate those millions the way prices do, no one knows. It has not been sustained at that scale, with tools we may not have invented yet. Not *it can't*. *It hasn't been tried.* And it is worth trying. The scales already standing are not one instrument: a fridge on a sidewalk is a gift, seventy thousand workers a federation, thirty-five million a state — and Kerala is not a commons in the strict sense but a government that chose the floor. One refusal runs through all of them: that what we need to live should not be enclosed. Whether the commons grows beside the market or past it, we do not yet know. We know it grows.
