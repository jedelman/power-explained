The fourth is social — tracking always has been. You learn from the ones who walked this ground before you; storytelling is the oldest part of the practice. So: why has the obvious alternative — seizing state power, reorganizing from above — failed so reliably? The tradition that had better answers said so at the time, and was buried for it. Not buried because it was wrong. Buried because it was threatening: it demonstrated that the commons doesn't need the party, the vanguard, or the state to function. Gramsci, Luxemburg, Kropotkin, Graeber, Bookchin — and the Zapatistas who arrived at the same architecture, because the conditions required it.
