Everyone in this book did what they did for the birds and the food and the music and the laughter and the friendships. For the particular quality of a summer evening when the work is done and the people you love are around you and the thing you built is humming. For the chance to look back at the people who did this before you — Rosa Luxemburg naming birds through prison bars, Paulo Freire's sugarcane worker writing his name, the employee telling the regulars about the meditation circle — and feel that you showed them respect. Not by winning. By continuing.
