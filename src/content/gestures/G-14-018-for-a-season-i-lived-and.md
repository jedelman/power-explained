For a season I lived and worked with a community at the edge of wilderness in the Pacific Northwest. I was not a visitor. I was a teacher, and I taught not through information but through affect — the only way this transmission works, and only within a community, never one on one. I taught honoring and blessing and singing. I taught witnessing, listening, speaking to plants and trees. I taught the stalker's gait that the children dropped into when the deer walked into camp — not as a skill but as a doorway. Evocation, passed the only way it passes. I learned from people who carried these ways, and from ancient trees that were older than the tradition I was learning. I learned with my body and my soul as well as my mind. I remembered my ancestors.
