The night herons appear in the prologue, in the first chapter, in the chapter on conscientization, in the land chapter, and in the epilogue — not by plan but by accumulation, the collaboration finding the thread and pulling it through. The chapter on conscientization corrected something the earlier chapters had assumed: the herons know about the road. They know it in the way that matters. That correction came from the collaboration, mid-pass, and it changed what the thread means. The Atlantic City neighborhood is introduced in chapter one, returns in chapter two, appears in chapter six as the neighborhood where the shipyard workers cannot anchor a constituency, returns in chapter fourteen where the woman from chapter two names the completion of the enclosure before the analysis did, and appears in chapter eighteen as part of the pattern Roy and Federici documented. Five appearances. Each one earning what the previous built. The singularity argument runs from chapter eight's Wynter (the overrepresentation of Man as the system that cannot hold the one who thinks differently), through chapter ten's Luxemburg (naming singularity as the political condition of freedom), through the Turing section (the knowledge was useful, the person was a problem), through chapter sixteen's Simondon (the pre-individual field as the structure that enables singularity without requiring uniformity), through chapter sixteen-b's training corpus argument (you are what the system cannot train on), through this chapter — not as a planted theme but as a structural logic that kept arriving because the material kept producing it.
