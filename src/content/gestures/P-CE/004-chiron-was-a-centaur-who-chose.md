Chiron was a centaur who chose differently. Where the others were violent, he was gentle. Where the others burned, he taught. He lived in a cave on Mount Pelion, and to that cave came the sons of heroes: Achilles, Heracles, Asclepius, Jason, Peleus. He taught them medicine and music. He taught them the hunt and the stars. He understood both the cultivated and the wild — he inhabited both, he *was* both — and he used that doubleness to prepare others for what they would face. He could teach the hero because he was not entirely the hero. He could teach the body because he was not entirely the body. The boundary was where his teaching happened.
