He had one eye — lost in a childhood accident. He read badly, in a monotone his new congregation found intricate and difficult to follow. His homilies were repetitive, hard to parse. The local parishioners asked the bishop to replace him within weeks of his arrival. The bishop didn't. And Arizmendi — as everyone called him — stayed.
