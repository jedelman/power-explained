**Wilhelm Reich** — *character armor · the orgone*

He was one of Freud's most gifted young clinicians, his heir-apparent, until they broke over the death drive — Reich insisting that self-destruction is not innate but learned, manufactured in the authoritarian family where love comes conditioned on submission. He found the proof in his patients' bodies: histories carried in raised shoulders and held jaws, defenses set so early the person mistook them for character. He called it *character armor*, and argued in *The Mass Psychology of Fascism* (1933) that an armored body is the raw material of fascism. The Nazis banned the book; the German Communist Party expelled him the same year, because the diagnosis worked on Stalinists too. He fled, and went on putting his hands on the armor. In America he built the orgone accumulator — a box meant to gather a healing life energy. The box did not work; the diagnosis did. In 1956 a federal judge ordered his writings burned. He died in 1957 in a federal penitentiary, about a day before he was eligible to apply for parole.
