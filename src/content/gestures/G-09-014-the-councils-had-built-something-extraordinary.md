The councils had built something extraordinary inside the factories. They were right — everything about them was right. Workers could govern production. The hierarchy was unnecessary. The demonstration had been made.

And still they fell. Not because they were wrong but because they were isolated. Around them, in the churches and the schools and the newspapers and the forms of daily life, the terrain was organized around assumptions that made the councils seem like a temporary aberration rather than the beginning of something. When the industrial moment passed, the terrain reasserted itself. They had won the factories and built nothing in the ground that surrounds factories.

This was the failure. Not of will or organization or courage. Of territory. And it is the specific failure that the dominant left tradition has kept repeating across a century — winning the state, or the party, or the union, or the moment, and then finding that the terrain of civil society reconstitutes the old order under the new institutional forms. The armor falls. The consent rebuilds it.
