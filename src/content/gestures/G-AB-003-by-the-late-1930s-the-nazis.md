By the late 1930s the Nazis had placed Klee in the *Entartete Kunst* exhibition — *degenerate art* — alongside dozens of artists whose work the regime intended to erase from public memory. The angel had become a kind of mascot for the wrong side of an argument the regime was winning. Benjamin took the print with him when he fled.
