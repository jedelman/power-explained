He understood that intellectual property law was a system of incantations — words on paper, in the right form, with the right authority, that restructured reality by making ownership real. The standard incantation said: the creator controls what happens to this. Others may use it only on the creator's terms. It is the same speech-magic the early chapters named: common sense cast on the mind, ownership cast onto paper — both turning *the way things are* into *the way things must be*.
