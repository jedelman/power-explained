The people who designed this road were not bad people. The people who maintain it are not bad people. The city planners who have absorbed its existence as a given are not bad people. They are operating inside a template — a set of assumptions about what infrastructure is for, who it serves, what counts as a problem worth solving — that feels so natural it doesn't feel like a template at all. It feels like reality.
