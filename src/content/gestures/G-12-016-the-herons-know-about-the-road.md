The herons know about the road. They know it in the way that matters: they have adapted to it, they navigate around it, they persist in relationship with this particular stand of willow oaks despite it. The relationship between the herons and this place is older than the infrastructure that nearly severed it, and it encodes a knowledge of that place that no traffic study produced. The commons doesn't require our vocabulary to function. It requires the conditions. When the conditions are destroyed, it is not because the commons lacked a theory of itself — it is because the thing doing the destroying had the power to ignore what it couldn't see. This is what Roy was standing in the river to name.
