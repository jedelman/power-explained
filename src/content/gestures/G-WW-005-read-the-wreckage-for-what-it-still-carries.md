The second capacity is reading. Not reading books — reading what is in front of you. The wreckage of every cleared lot, every closed restaurant, every meadow that almost got mowed. The angel looks at the pile of debris and refuses to look away; the wizard looks at the same pile and asks what in it is still good. A chip of cedar from a tree that should not have been cut, kept above a bar and then on a shelf at home. A pattern of cooking that survived a kitchen's closing because three people learned the knife. The reading is not nostalgia. It is the discipline of seeing what carried through, so that what carried through can carry on.
