The awareness practices are political acts. The walk around the neighborhood is political. Waking up for the cardinal at 5am. Finding the heron nests and telling them you love them — this is political. Not because it changes the zoning board or restores the common land or forgives the debt — but because it trains the attention toward what is real and living and shared, and attention trained that way is the root of everything else. The enclosure underneath all the other enclosures: you don't need to suppress the conclusion if you can enclose the attention that would produce it. The awareness practices are the counter-enclosure. They are what makes it possible to see the trickle when the catastrophe has trained you to look only for the flood.
