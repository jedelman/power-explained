The deep memory has a name: the CNT. The Confederación Nacional del Trabajo — anarcho-syndicalist, federated, assembly-based; at its 1930s peak, over a million members in Catalonia. The CNT-FAI ran Barcelona in 1936. Taking it down took fascists and Communists, both — Franco's army from outside the Republic, and inside the Republic the Stalinist-aligned forces attacking the anarchist and POUM positions in Barcelona's May Days of 1937. Different uniforms, same intention. By the time the war ended, what was left of the CNT was driven underground, killed off, scattered into exile.

The form survived. Murray Bookchin took his lessons from it and called the practice *libertarian municipalism*. The Barcelona coalitions of the twenty-first century take theirs from the same place.

The CNT was Spain's part of a wider European left of the 1920s and 30s — Gramsci writing in a Mussolini prison, Reich in Berlin naming the somatic-political fascism of the era. The CNT was the part of that generation that built the most actual power. They lost by being defeated, not by being wrong.
