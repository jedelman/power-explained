What happened when the deer walked through camp was an example of *magic*. We'll see many more throughout this book. Magic is what happens when particular things meet and become, briefly, a single thing that was not there before. The deer carried *the secret ways* — the forest's irreducible particularity that no grid can map. Each child carried *the gift* — their singular angle on the world, the body they had inherited from generations whose names they don't know. I had taught them the stalking gait earlier that week. When the deer came, the body took over — and the body remembers things the mind has not yet caught up to. The body is what you have in common with your ancestors. When the deer appeared, what each child uniquely carried met what the deer carried and met what the other children carried, and for twenty minutes, the four of them — deer, children, morning, silence — became one thing. That is magic. It is the felt recognition of a composition coming together, like the climax of a symphony when the chord that has been gathering finally arrives and you feel it before you can name it.
