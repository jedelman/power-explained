*Eldritch* originally meant: of the supernatural, the strange, the unearthly. But in Old English the root is *elf-kingdom* — not horror but the other country, the one adjacent to this one, accessed through the wrong door or the lucky stumble. Not the absence of nature but a different nature. Hybrid. Otherworldly in the sense that it belongs to a world continuous with this one but not reducible to it.
