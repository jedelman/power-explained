Address it, if you can do so without feeling entirely ridiculous. Not performatively — you don't need to make a speech. But as you might acknowledge a neighbor you are passing on the sidewalk. A nod. A word. Something that communicates: *I see that you are here.*

And then notice what happens next.

This is not a guarantee. The bird may leave. The tree will not respond in any way you can immediately measure. The creek doesn't care whether you've acknowledged it. But you are not trying to produce a result. You are trying to restore a channel that has been closed by the habit of treating the living world as backdrop.
