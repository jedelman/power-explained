Silvia Federici has spent the later part of her career pursuing what she calls *re-enchanting the world* — and she means something precise by it. The disenchantment of the world was not a natural process. It was a political project. The witch was not burned because medieval people were superstitious and Renaissance people were not. The witch was burned because she maintained a relationship to the land, to the body, to the cycles of birth and death and season, that made her community less dependent on the emerging market. She knew things — about plants, about illness, about birth, about grief — that the market could not sell, because she gave them freely, and because they were embedded in relationship rather than procedure. You could not extract the knowledge from her and sell it as a service. You could only destroy it or absorb it.
