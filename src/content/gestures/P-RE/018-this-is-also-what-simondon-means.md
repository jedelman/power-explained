This is also what Simondon means by the pre-individual field. Shared knowing that emerges from genuine encounter between different perceivers, each bringing what the other cannot access. The commons of perception is as real as the commons of land. It can be built. It can be cultivated. It can be enclosed.

It is enclosed every time the curriculum decides there is only one correct answer. Every time the performance review substitutes measurable output for genuine contact. Every time the meeting runs a script that produces consensus rather than encounter. The enclosure of perception is the impoverishment of the collective mind. We live inside it so thoroughly we have forgotten what unenclosed feels like.
