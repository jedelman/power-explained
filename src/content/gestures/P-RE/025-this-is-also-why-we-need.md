This is also why we need each other. The Far Outside requires a companion. Not because you can't go alone — you can, and sometimes must — but because what you find there needs to be received by someone who is not you, or it cannot be fully real. Prechtel says it directly: the soul's gift is not for the soul that carries it. It is for the community that receives it. The shaman returns from the underground not to keep what they found, but because there are people on the surface who need it. The direction of travel is always: back.
