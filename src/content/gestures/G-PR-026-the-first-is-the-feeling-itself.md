The first is the felt sense — the tension in the gut or the shoulders before any thought arrives, paired with a recognition: *this is a pattern.* You know something is wrong. The machine produces the same outcomes regardless of who runs it, and you have felt this in your body before you had words for it. Part I gives you the words, starting with a crossing in Norfolk, Virginia, and the neighborhood demolished to make it.
