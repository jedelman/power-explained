That network is the care commons: the accumulated relational intelligence — who can be trusted, who knows what, who shows up — that capital did not produce and cannot reproduce, but has always depended on. The enclosure of it is not metaphorical. The pricing-out of communities, the underpayment of care workers, the criminalization of mutual support: these are mechanisms for capturing what communities built, by making it legible as a service to be purchased rather than a commons to be governed.
