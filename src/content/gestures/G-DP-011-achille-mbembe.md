**Achille Mbembe** — *necropolitics*

He was born in Otélé, Cameroon, in 1957 — the year France was actively suppressing the Union des Populations du Cameroun, the nationalist movement demanding independence. His father was Ruben Um Nyobè, the founding leader of the UPC, shot dead by French troops in 1958, the year after Mbembe was born. He was educated by Dominicans near Douala, became active in student politics, and went to Paris, completing his doctorate at the Sorbonne under the historian Catherine Coquery-Vidrovitch while immersing himself in Foucault, Derrida, Deleuze, and Lacan — learning the colonizer's most sophisticated tools, and what they could and couldn't see. He later returned to Africa, to Dakar, as executive director of CODESRIA; met the scholar Sarah Nuttall at a conference in Nairobi in 1997; wrote *On the Postcolony* during a Cape Town interlude; and moved to Johannesburg, where both took positions at WISER.
