None of the six is a step. There is no order in which you do them. In any actual room, on any actual day, all six are running at once or none of them are — you name what is happening while you read the room while you hold what cannot be set down while you build whatever small thing the room needs while you enter the part of the room nobody is looking at, and you do all of that while continuing to be there. The walking is what gathers them. You will not get good at them in sequence. You will get good at them together, because that is the only way they are ever asked of you. Which means the only practice that ever trains the walking is the walking.
