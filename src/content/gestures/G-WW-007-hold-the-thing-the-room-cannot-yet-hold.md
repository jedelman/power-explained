The fourth capacity is holding. The body that does not slam shut when something hard comes in. The breath that goes all the way to the bottom even when the room would prefer you to keep it shallow. The face that can hear what is actually being said without rearranging itself into a more comfortable expression. The orgone chapter named the cost of the armor; the holding is what the un-armored body can finally do. Hold the grief in the room. Hold the question that has no good answer. Hold the silence after the bad news, long enough for someone else to find their footing. The wizard is not the one with answers. The wizard is the one whose body can stay where most bodies leave.
