I've been to cities where planners made different assumptions — where pedestrian movement was the design constraint and car throughput was the variable, where crossing a major street took thirty seconds instead of three minutes, where a neighborhood was understood as something that should not be bisected by six lanes of arterial traffic. Once you've been to those places, you can't un-know them. The alternative becomes thinkable. The rage becomes possible.
