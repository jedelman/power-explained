She was arrested. She lectured from prison. She held, even held. By the time she was forty she had spent roughly a third of her adult life in jail. In 1913 she published *The Accumulation of Capital*, and the party and the Marxist establishment attacked it — because it was an argument they didn't want to hear, and because she was right.

Her argument: capitalism can't sustain itself from its own interior. She had read the terrain and seen its seams.
