On the plantation, a human being is classified as property. Every hour of labor goes to the owner — no wages, no negotiation, no legally recognized counter-power. Reproduction happens at no cost to the system: the enslaved person's body produces the next generation of workers, and that labor too is uncompensated, expropriated, invisible in the accounting. Risk is externalized completely. This is capitalism at its logical extreme: total externalization of reproduction costs, total suppression of any counter-power that labor could build.
