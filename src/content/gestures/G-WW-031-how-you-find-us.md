You will find us the way we found each other. By becoming the kind of person who recognizes the room when they walk into it. By saying the second sentence at the bar without being asked. By showing up to the meadow with a notebook because someone has to start writing down what is growing there. By naming, in the kitchen of someone you barely know, what the kitchen has been quietly trying to say to you both. You find us by becoming someone we recognize on sight, which is the same as saying you find us by becoming the next us. The door opens for you, and behind you it quietly stops opening the other way. You will not be able to go back to the version of yourself the book first met; you will be the version the next reader is going to need to find.
