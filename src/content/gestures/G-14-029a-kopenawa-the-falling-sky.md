The threat in its operational form kills the people who defend the land — but the defense also speaks. Davi Kopenawa, a Yanomami shaman born around 1956 near the Rio Toototobi, has spent decades campaigning to expel illegal gold miners and rubber tappers from Yanomami land; he co-founded the Hutukara Yanomami Association in 2004; following his international campaigning, Brazil recognized Yanomami land in 1992; and he has faced death threats for it. What sets his case apart is that it reaches us in his own words. *The Falling Sky*, told to and composed with the anthropologist Bruce Albert, defends the forest and the standing to speak for it at once. The miners take the land; the habit of studying from outside takes the account of it. He refuses both. The title is his: in Yanomami cosmology, the whites' extraction threatens to bring the sky down.
