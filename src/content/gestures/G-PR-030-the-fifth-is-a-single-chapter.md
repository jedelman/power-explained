The fifth is the elder's question — the one that makes you stand up straighter and choose your best words, and then sends you back to the spot to see what you didn't see the first time. A single chapter, the pivot of the book. Two people working from the outside of the dominant framework: Arundhati Roy standing waist-deep in a rising river, naming what the dam project's accounting couldn't see. Paulo Freire's sugarcane worker writing his name and understanding, for the first time, that the arrangement had been made. The shift from *this is natural* to *this was made* is already the beginning of action. And the commons of consciousness — the shared capacity to perceive clearly — is both what enclosure most needs to take and what this book is trying to help restore.
