**Peter Kropotkin** — *mutual aid · the circle in the blizzard*

He was a prince who became a geographer who became an anarchist. Born in Moscow in 1842 into one of the oldest families in Russia — the Kropotkins were descended from the Rurik dynasty, the original princes of Rus — he was educated at the Corps of Pages, the most elite military school in the empire, and appointed personal page to Tsar Alexander II. He could have had any career the Russian aristocracy offered. He chose Siberia: five years in the 1860s mapping glaciers and observing animal populations across one of the harshest environments on earth, where what he found contradicted everything the dominant theory said about nature. Reading Huxley's "gladiatorial show" from the field, he found it inaccurate — the species that survived the Siberian winters were the most cooperative, not the most competitive. He spent the next twenty years building the evidence. *Mutual Aid: A Factor of Evolution* was published in 1902.
