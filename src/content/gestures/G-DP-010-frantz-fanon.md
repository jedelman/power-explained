**Frantz Fanon** — *colonized consciousness · the wretched*

He grew up in Fort-de-France, Martinique, in a middle-class Creole family raised on the French assimilation project — the promise that civilization was achievable by anyone willing to adopt its forms. He was fourteen when Martinique came under Vichy control and the officers' casual racism made legible what the promise had been organized to hide. He volunteered for Free France at eighteen, fought in Alsace, was decorated, then went to Lyon for medical school, where he read Aimé Césaire — fascism as colonialism come home — and Sartre, Hegel, Merleau-Ponty. *Black Skin, White Masks* (1952) was that framework in its first form. In 1953 he arrived at Blida-Joinville Hospital outside Algiers as a psychiatrist, and within months was treating both sides of the colony. He was thirty-five and dying of leukemia when *The Wretched of the Earth* was dictated to his wife Josie in ten weeks.
