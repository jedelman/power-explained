There is a name I have for the work. I have used it carefully, with people I trust, because it sounds like it could be misread. The name is *psychic architecture*. What I mean by it is the thing I just described — the sculpting of a way of being for other people to inhabit, so that some particular kind of attention becomes possible in a room, and then in the rooms that room opens onto. A restaurant is psychic architecture. A reading group is psychic architecture. A wine-bar staff who, at the after-hours bar, will spontaneously explain to a stranger why we don't sell what we don't sell — that is the structure I was trying to build with the materials I had. It is what the wizard does.
