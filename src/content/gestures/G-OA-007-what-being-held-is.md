The floor was solitude. Not chosen solitude, not the rich kind — the kind where you are alone with a mind that has turned on you, in a body that has stopped answering, and the aloneness and the collapse feed each other in a loop with no door in it. The woods were not solitude. The woods were being *held*, which is a different physical state, and the body knows the difference immediately, the way it knows warm from cold, before the part of you that explains things has been consulted at all.

This is the inversion of everything Reich saw, and it is the reason the box could never have worked. If the armored body is the raw material of fascism, then the un-armored body *in community* is the thing fascism cannot use. Not the individual healed in private — that is the box, sealed and alone, and the box does not work. The thing that works is the person held by a group of others in a real place, doing real things with their hands, eating from the same pot, kept warm by a fire that belongs to no one. The armor comes off not because you decided to take it off — you cannot decide your way out of armor, deciding is more holding — but because, for the first time in years, the body looks around at the others and concludes, correctly, that it is safe enough to set the holding down.
