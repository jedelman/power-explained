Not a performance — a class, or something between a class and a party, the boundary dissolving quickly. People of all ages. Couples, groups of friends, laughter, a few people alone at the edges bobbing along, already getting drawn in. The permanent covered stalls along the street sold something cold and sweet. The street, which an hour earlier had been about fish, was now about dancing, and nobody had organized this except in the loosest possible sense — someone had brought a bike and a sound system and an idea, and the city had made the rest possible. Music made the street a commons — evoked it, not organized it: the oldest magic, felt before it is understood.
