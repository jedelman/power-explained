  Luxemburg wrote back the same year. She said this idea was backwards,
  and being backwards would lead to disaster. She said this: if you
  build a revolution on the idea that the leaders know better than the
  members, you do not get a tool that sets people free. You get a fat
  layer of bosses. Those bosses crush the very thing that makes a real
  revolution: the free, spread-out, local ideas that workers come up
  with on their own. The bosses crush all that and just demand that
  people obey the party. She said the small leading group does not
  lead the workers. It takes their place. And taking their place is
  not freedom. It is ruling over them while meaning well.


  She was not against people working together in groups. She was
  against one way of building the group: a way that sends all
  knowledge and power to the top. A way that treats the smart ideas of
  millions of people as raw stuff to be handled by a party office. A
  way that cannot learn from the people it claims to speak for,
  because it has already decided it knows more than they do.

Luxemburg's response, published the same year: this is backwards, and the backwardness is catastrophic. If you build a revolutionary organization on the premise that leadership knows better than members, you don't get an instrument of liberation. You get a bureaucracy that suppresses precisely the spontaneous, distributed, locally-rooted political creativity of workers — the creativity that produces genuine revolutionary capacity — in favor of party discipline. The vanguard doesn't lead the working class. It substitutes for it. And substitution is not liberation. Substitution is domination with good intentions.

She was not arguing against organization. She was arguing against a specific architecture: one that routes all knowledge and authority to the top, that treats the distributed intelligence of millions of people as raw material to be processed by a party headquarters, that cannot learn from the people it claims to represent because it has defined itself as already knowing more than they do.
