I ran the meeting. I cannot say I didn't conduct it well because I did. I felt, simultaneously, my heart leaping at the members — who had understood what the organization was supposed to be for and had used the rules to make it so — and sinking at the board, my peers, who sat in that room and watched the membership vote and then did nothing.
