Then it collapsed. The Italian Socialist Party leadership, afraid of where it was going, refused to extend the action nationally. The industrialists waited. Within two years, Benito Mussolini was in power. Within four, a young Sardinian journalist and Communist Party leader named Antonio Gramsci was arrested.

He was thirty-five years old. Mussolini's prosecutor told the court: *we must stop this brain from functioning for twenty years.*

What happened instead is that the brain, supplied with books and exercise notebooks under prison censorship, produced nearly three thousand pages of analysis over the next six years [FLAG: "the next six years" | Writing began February 1929, three years after arrest, and ended 1935 — six years of active writing is accurate, but the phrase "next six years" post-trial (1928) could mislead; technically correct if counting 1929–1935] — before his health failed and the writing stopped. He died in 1937, six days after his conditional release, his body destroyed by eleven years of confinement. [CORRECTION: "four days" → "six days" | Gramsci was conditionally released April 21, 1937, and died April 27, 1937 — six days later] He was forty-six.
