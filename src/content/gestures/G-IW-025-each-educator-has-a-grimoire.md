Each educator has a spellbook. Not a printed book — a personal one, accumulated over years. Each wizard's is different. The wizard who has done the work long enough has gathered the small techniques for loosening what binds, and for calling on what helps: when to speak and when to wait, which silences are productive and which are awkward, what acts on a five-year-old's attention and what releases it. The curriculum does not produce the demons. It encounters them — as anyone does — in bodies that have already been bound by them. Each demon has strengths as well as weaknesses; the work of the spellbook is to know both, so you know when to push and when to step aside. There are spirits in the spellbook too — the forces that act with you, the allies the wizard learns to recognize and invite. The deer was a spirit. Each spirit has its own conditions for arriving and its own ways of being received; the work of the spellbook is to know both, so you know when to call and when to wait. The spellbook is not secret. It is informal. It is also yours, alone, to make. Anyone who reads this book and decides to start their own spellbook will find the others doing the same. The networks exist. You follow the thread.
