Address it, if you can do so without feeling entirely ridiculous. Not performatively — you don't need to make a speech. As you might acknowledge a neighbor you are passing on the sidewalk. A nod. A word. Something that communicates: *I see that you are here.*

Notice what happens next.

This is not a guarantee. The bird may leave. The tree will not respond in any way you can immediately measure. The creek doesn't care whether you've acknowledged it. You are not trying to produce a result. You are restoring a channel that has been closed by the habit of treating the living world as backdrop.
