Some of the armor I am describing did not come from the ten years in software. It came from much earlier, from a childhood in the South, from a particular training in what a body is allowed to expect. The book has a name for the result already — the shrug, the posture of diminished expectation, the way a body can learn the ceiling of the possible before the child it belongs to has even thought to ask the question. I described it from the outside in an earlier chapter, as a thing you can see on a street in Richmond. I did not describe it from the inside, which is the only place I actually know it from.
