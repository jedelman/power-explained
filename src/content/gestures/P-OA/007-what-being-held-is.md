The floor was solitude. Not chosen solitude, not the rich kind — the kind where you are alone with a mind that has turned on you, in a body that has stopped answering, and the aloneness and the collapse feed each other in a loop with no door in it. The woods were not solitude. The woods were being *held*, which is a different physical state and the body knows the difference immediately, the way it knows warm from cold, before the part of you that explains things has been consulted at all.

{{NEED: OA-02 — the first week in the woods. What happened on day 3 or day 5 that your body registered as different, before you had words for it? Was it a meal someone made? A specific person? Something said, or pointedly not said? The quality of the silence around a fire? I want one concrete thing the body clocked as "this is different from the floor" — the moment, not the conclusion.}}

This is the inversion of everything Reich saw. If the armored body alone is the raw material of fascism, the un-armored body *in community* is the thing it cannot use. Not the individual healed in private — that is the box, and the box does not work. The person held by a group of others in a real place, doing real things with their hands, eating from the same pot, kept warm by a fire nobody owns. The armor comes off not because you decided to take it off but because for the first time in years the body looks around and concludes, correctly, that it is safe enough to stop holding.
