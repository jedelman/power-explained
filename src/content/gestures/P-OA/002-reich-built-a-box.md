Wilhelm Reich built a box. He called it an orgone accumulator — a cabinet of layered metal and organic material that he believed concentrated a life energy he named *orgone*, and that a person could sit inside to be restored. The box does not work. There is no orgone. The United States government burned his books and his research and let him die in a federal penitentiary in 1957 for selling the boxes across state lines, which is its own kind of answer to the question of whether the powerful find the un-armored body threatening. I am not asking you to believe in the box. I am asking you to notice that a man who spent his life studying how power gets into the body ended up building a container whose only real ingredient was being held — sealed in, surrounded, kept warm, left alone to feel. He was wrong about the energy and right about the need. People came to the box because they were starving for exactly one thing, and it was not electricity.
