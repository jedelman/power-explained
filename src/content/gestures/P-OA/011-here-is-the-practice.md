So here is the practice, and it is not a regimen, because the floor taught me to distrust anyone who hands a collapsed person a regimen. It is smaller than that. Once today, notice where you are holding. The jaw is a good place to start, or the shoulders, or the breath you take in shallow so as not to feel the bottom of it. Do not try to fix it. Trying to fix it is more holding. Just locate one place where the armor is, and let the locating be the whole of it. That is the first half. The second half is harder and you cannot do it alone, which is the point: notice the next time you are in the presence of other people and your body lets a held thing go without your permission — the shoulders that drop when a particular person walks in, the breath that finally reaches the bottom around a particular table. Mark that. That table, that person, that fire — that is where the medicine is kept. The enclosure has spent a great deal of money convincing you the medicine is the box. It is not the box. It was never the box. It is the people around it.
