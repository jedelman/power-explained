I want to tell you how I learned that the armor is real, which is that mine failed. I had spent about ten years in software, in the dissociation — that is the only honest word for it — of being very good at a thing that asked me to not be in my body at all. And the failure did not arrive as a crisis. It arrived as a slope. I got slower. I stumbled more — not on the floor, on the work, on the simplest tasks, the way you stumble over a word you have said ten thousand times. And one day I stumbled and I could not get back up, so I lay down. I lay down for four months.
