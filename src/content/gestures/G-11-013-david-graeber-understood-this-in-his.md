David Graeber understood this in his bones because it had happened to him. He organized the horizontal decision-making networks that were themselves the argument — not a tactic toward a goal but the goal, practiced in the middle of a mobilization. He argued that most jobs in the modern economy were, by the assessment of the people doing them, entirely pointless, and then conducted wildly unorthodox field research in public on the people his argument had hit a nerve with. The book that came from it is still causing arguments. I was caught in one on Bluesky myself.
