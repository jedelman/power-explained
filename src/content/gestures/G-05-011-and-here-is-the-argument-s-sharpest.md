And here is the argument's sharpest edge: this doesn't end. Every outside absorbed creates the need for the next one. Every commons enclosed generates the conditions requiring the next enclosure. When the geographical frontier closes, the machine finds new outsides — debt, intellectual property, the privatization of water and seed and genetic code, the digital enclosure of attention and behavior. The frontier moves. The need for a frontier never disappears.
