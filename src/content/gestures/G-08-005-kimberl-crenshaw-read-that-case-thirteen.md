Kimberlé Crenshaw read that case thirteen years later and named the gap. She called it intersectionality. But intersectionality was not primarily a legal concept. It was a structural argument: the frameworks we build to address oppression are built around particular experiences, and the people whose oppression arises from the *combination* of categories fall through. The gap is not a crack in an otherwise coherent system. It is the system working as designed — for the people whose experiences shaped its design, against the people whose experiences the design couldn't see.
