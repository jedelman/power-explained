The hospital that was built on Atlantic City now trains the doctors who serve the region. It has produced significant medical research. It has received significant public investment and genuine community pride. What it does not produce is any acknowledgment of the community whose displacement made it possible. The neighborhood is not commemorated. The residents who were removed are not named. The land simply became institutional, and the institution proceeded, and this is understood as progress.

This is enclosure in its full form. Not just the taking — the forgetting. The conversion is complete when the commons that was destroyed becomes invisible as a commons, when what replaced it is understood as having grown on empty ground.
