*Biopolitics* is the name for the contest. Not the apparatus, not the effect — the fight between them.

Deleuze and Guattari describe something like this in *A Thousand Plateaus*: the inscription of strata, layer on layer, each layer a record of the pressures that produced it. The refrain — a bird's territorial song — as inscription, as the daily renegotiation of where things stand. Not evolution. Evolutionary.

Think of a forest. Every creature in it is attending to and adjusting to the patterns of every other creature. Coyotes noting where the cougars have been. Wolves reading the wind for elk. Birds renegotiating territories through song, every morning, without pause. Ants laying pheromone trails that aggregate into roads. Bears pressing the same routes for generations until the trail becomes the landscape.

No one designed the trail system. The trail system is biopolitics: the accumulated inscription of every creature tracking and being tracked, moving and being moved. It is how the forest knows itself.

Gramsci writing in the prison cell was doing biopolitics. Freire teaching adults to read the word as the reading of the world was doing biopolitics. Picking up this book is doing biopolitics.
