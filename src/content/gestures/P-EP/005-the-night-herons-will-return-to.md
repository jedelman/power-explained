The night herons will return to the willow oaks in a few weeks. Every spring, up from Central America, to raise their young in the same trees. We find the nests. We tell them we love them and wish them good luck. They hear us — they talk to each other about it, in ways we can only begin to understand. They know about the road too, in ways we can't imagine. They've been navigating it for years. Their territory maps include the crossing. The people who bless the nests are part of what they know about this place.
