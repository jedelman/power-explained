It's taken me years to learn to quiet the subtle movements of my body enough that they feel safe this close. I've watched children learn it in a week. The doves don't know what I'm writing. They don't know about the enclosure, the commons, the seventeen chapters of argument that came before this page. They are just here, on the ground, in the way that living things are here in the morning.
