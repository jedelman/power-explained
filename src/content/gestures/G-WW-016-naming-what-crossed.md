Nothing about me had changed in that moment. I had been the same person at the start of the conversation as at the end. What had changed was that the thing I had been carrying alone — the reason we sold the wine, the politics under the aesthetic — was now also being carried by someone who had not been told to carry it. That is the crossing. It is not a graduation. It is a redistribution. You realize, by way of someone else's spontaneous fluency, that you have been a guide for some unknown length of time without noticing the day it started. And once you know it, the question of the work changes. It stops being *am I doing this right* and becomes *what does the room need from me now that I am not the only one doing this*.
