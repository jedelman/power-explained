One of the things they sang me toward was my own darkness. Song was how that community made culture — each one with a lineage, a way it was offered and passed down — and most were what you would expect: campfire songs, folk songs, old protest songs from movements before ours. I am a metalhead. I did not know if my songs would be welcome. But over the year I found a place for them — acoustic Electric Wizard, Windhand, Bathory. If you have never heard "Mother Earth, Father Thunder" as a sing-along, it is far more powerful than you would think. What it opened, for all of us, was a way to look straight at the dark — the grief and the pain the work turns up — and not flinch. Not the songs. Us, collectively making a home for them: welcoming the grief and the agony by singing it together, turning it into something like strength. It was a longing I did not know I had, and never expected anyone to meet. Until they did.
