The commons is not a project to be completed. It is a practice to be continued. The practice that survives is the one that has moved from the leader into the institution, into the culture, into the daily life of the people who carry it forward without knowing they are carrying it forward — the employee telling the story at the bar, the child who stalked the deer, the friend who finds the heron nests every spring without having been asked to.
