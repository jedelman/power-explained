**Murray Bookchin** — *social ecology · hierarchy*

He was born in the Bronx in 1921, the son of Jewish anarchist immigrants, and he grew up in a neighborhood that was itself a kind of argument — dense, multilingual, poor, and alive, built around the mutual aid infrastructure that immigrant communities construct wherever they land: the landsmanshaftn, the fraternal societies, the credit unions, the cooperative apartment buildings, the clubs where people argued in Yiddish and Italian and Spanish about how the world should be organized. Then Robert Moses spent thirty years destroying it; the Cross Bronx Expressway alone displaced sixty thousand people. Bookchin synthesized what he was watching into a theory he called *Social Ecology*: the domination of nature and the domination of human beings by each other are not separate problems but share a single root — hierarchy. You cannot solve the ecological crisis without dismantling it.
