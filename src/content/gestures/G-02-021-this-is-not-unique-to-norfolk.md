This is not unique to Norfolk or to neighborhood governance. It is the operating logic of participatory processes designed to absorb participation rather than transmit it into power. The petition that gets added to your email list. The public comment that goes into the record and changes nothing. The community meeting that produces a report that produces another community meeting.
