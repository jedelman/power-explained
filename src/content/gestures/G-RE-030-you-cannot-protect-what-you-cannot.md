You cannot protect what you cannot imagine losing. You cannot imagine losing what you have never met. The environmental movement's crisis is partly this: not that people don't care, but that the thing they care about is increasingly abstract. A statistic. A projection. A graph. You cannot grieve a graph. You can grieve a meadow you have walked through every morning, whose specific birds you know, whose specific smell you carry in your body. The relationship makes the loss real. The loss being real makes action possible.

This is not sentimentality. This is the epistemology of care.
