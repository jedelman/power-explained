The political implication is the one that unsettles everyone: decolonization is not admission to the category of the human as currently defined. It is the reconstruction of that category from the ground up. Rupture, at the deepest possible scale — the code of *Man* itself broken, so a different human can become. Development economics that treats Western capitalist modernization as the destination for everyone else embeds the overrepresented Man as its implicit norm. Educational curricula that treat European intellectual history as the curriculum and everything else as regional addition do the same. These are the overrepresentation of Man institutionalized, running so deep it feels like reality rather than choice.
