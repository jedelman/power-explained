One afternoon, a man in his late fifties — a sugarcane worker who had never gone to school, who had spent his adult life in conditions the landowning class described as natural and inevitable — first wrote his own name, then wrote the word for the system under which he worked, then said out loud, in something like surprise: *I made this. This arrangement — I didn't make it. It was made. By specific people, for specific reasons.*
