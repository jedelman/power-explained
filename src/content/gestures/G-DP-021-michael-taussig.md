**Michael Taussig** — *terror and healing*

Anthropologist, born 1940 in Sydney, Australia, and professor of anthropology at Columbia University; a Marxist critic who reads Marx's commodity fetishism through Walter Benjamin and holds that anthropology's principal concern should be to critique Western capitalist culture. In *Shamanism, Colonialism, and the Wild Man*, he reads the Putumayo rubber-boom atrocities against Indigenous people — the "culture of terror," the "space of death" — and shows that colonial extraction produced both the terror and the "wild," magical image of the colonized, projected by the West and pinned back on its victims. His turn is that the shaman he follows (named in the book as José García) harnessed that projected wildness and made it a power to heal: the colonizer's own image, turned back.
