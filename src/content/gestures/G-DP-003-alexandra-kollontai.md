**Alexandra Kollontai** — *women in power · The Workers' Opposition*

She named, from inside the Soviet government, what was happening to it: the party had substituted itself for the working class. She made the case in print — *The Workers' Opposition*, published in Pravda in January 1921 — arguing that the unions themselves, not the party's bureaucrats, should build the new economy, and that a party that could no longer hear its own workers was already becoming the thing it had overthrown. The Tenth Congress answered in March, banning the pamphlet and dissolving her faction in the same stroke. Rather than wait to be expelled, she wrote to Stalin and asked to be sent abroad. From 1922 she served in the Soviet missions — Norway first, one of the first women in modern diplomacy — kept useful, kept visible, and kept permanently away from the rooms where what she had named was completing itself. She made Gramsci's diagnosis eight years before the prison notebooks, and outlived him by fifteen years.
