The first is *reading*. The terrain is what you read. When I'm in the field with a group, I'm paying attention to what everyone around me is paying attention to — and also to what they're not attending to. I'm listening to what people are saying and what they're not saying, watching how their bodies agree or disagree with their words. I'm listening to the birds, who are commenting on our collective energy and also on things I can't perceive yet. I'm feeling the moisture in the air, noticing how ambient sound and light shift, tracking how all of this affects the people around me. All of this at the same time. This is not exceptional — it's what human beings are built to do. We've just been trained out of it.
