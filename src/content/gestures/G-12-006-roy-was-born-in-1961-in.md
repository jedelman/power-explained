Roy was born in 1961 in Shillong. Her father was Bengali Hindu; her mother was a Kerala Christian who had sued her own brother for the right to inherit property — a case that took years and won, and that became the origin of a Kerala law extending daughters' inheritance rights. [CORRECTION: "sued her own father" → "sued her own brother" | Mary Roy sued her brother George Isaac after their father P.V. Isaac died. The case was Mary Roy v. State of Kerala (1986).] Roy grew up knowing that the arrangement you were living under was something that had been built, and could be challenged, and sometimes changed.
