The wages of whiteness don't require belief. They don't require conscious calculation. They require only the ambient knowledge of which spaces are yours and which ones require you to manage something. The body knows before the mind. The neighborhood meeting activated that management. The dive bar didn't. It is a social wage paid in the currency of ease, and its cost is paid by the people whose presence at the neighborhood meeting you were, at some level, afraid of.
