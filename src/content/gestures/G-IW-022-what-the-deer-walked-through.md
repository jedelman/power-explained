What the deer walked through, what the children moved on, what the educator had spent years coming to know — was the same thing. The forest. The state park's edges. The curriculum the educator had to rebuild for five-year-olds. The book calls this *the terrain*. The terrain is what the wizard reads. It is older than anyone walking on it; it produced them; they did not produce it. It has one fundamental axis: it has been enclosed, or it is held in common. The old-growth forest still stands at the commons end of that axis.
