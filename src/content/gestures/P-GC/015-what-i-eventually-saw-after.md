What I eventually saw — after he was gone, after the exhaustion lifted enough to let me look clearly — was that he relied entirely on an authoritarian mode of perception. Without explicit permission, instruction, and external discipline, he could not act on something he cared about. Not because he didn't care. Not because he lacked capability. Because the system that had produced him had produced him for a specific kind of world: one in which someone else always holds the frame.
