Stallman's insight was to cast the same incantation backward. He wrote the GNU General Public License — a document that used the grammar of ownership to *prevent* enclosure. Anyone who received the software could use it, modify it, share it — but could not enclose it. Anyone who passed it on had to pass it on under the same terms. The spell propagated its own resistance. Free to use, free to modify, free to distribute, but not free to close. The counter-spell in its most precise form: the same pact, cast for the commons.
