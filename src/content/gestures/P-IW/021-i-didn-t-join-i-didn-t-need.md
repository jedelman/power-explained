I didn't join. I didn't need to. What I needed was to be near it and understand why it exists. What I felt, standing there, was relief. The ease of knowing that life goes on even if yours doesn't. That the work of building the commons is larger than any one person's contribution, that the deer will walk through camp again next summer, that somewhere right now someone is sitting in the presence of an old tree and feeling, for the first time, what old means. The music doesn't need you to be there. The herons don't know about the road. Life continues. That knowledge — not as consolation but as fact — is what makes it possible to go back to the meeting, the crossing, the vote that passed and was ignored, and try again.
