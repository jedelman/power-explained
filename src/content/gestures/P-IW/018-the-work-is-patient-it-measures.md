The work is patient. It measures its effects in decades. It will not produce a policy victory next year. It may produce, twenty years from now, a generation of voters, organizers, parents, and officials who understand in their bodies what is at stake when an old-growth forest is auctioned for timber. That understanding, once embodied, is very hard to undo.
