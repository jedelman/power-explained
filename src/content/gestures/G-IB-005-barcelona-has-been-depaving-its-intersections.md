Barcelona has been depaving its intersections for years. [FLAG: "depaving" may overstate the physical intervention — the superblocks program typically begins with tactical urbanism (paint, planters, modular furniture) before any asphalt removal; some intersections have been physically resurfaced with permeable paving, but most transformations to date are not literal depaving | verify whether "depaving" is accurate for the specific intersections Jason observed, or whether a softer term like "reclaiming" or "transforming" is more precise] Taking back space that was given to cars in the 1960s and 1970s, returning it to the people who live on the streets that those cars cut through. The city calls them *superblocks* — clusters of streets deprioritized for through traffic, freed up for walking, cycling, planting, sitting, dancing. The policy was controversial when it started.
