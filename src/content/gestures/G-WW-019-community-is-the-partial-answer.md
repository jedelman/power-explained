There is a partial answer and I want to name it. Community helps. When the architecture is built in the open, with other people walking through it, the people considering whether to enter can see what entering has done to those already inside. They can watch someone proceed down their own path. They can see what someone three years in looks like, and ten years in, and what someone leaving looks like when they leave. None of that gives them full prior consent — they still cannot know from the outside what the inside will do to them. But it gives them more than nothing. It gives them other people's lived evidence. That is most of what consent ever was, in any tradition worth a damn. You see how the others walk. You decide whether to walk with them.
