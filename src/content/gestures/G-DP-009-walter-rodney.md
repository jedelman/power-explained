**Walter Rodney** — *underdevelopment*

He was from Guyana, educated in Jamaica and London, his PhD in African history completed at twenty-four. He spent the rest of his life teaching and organizing — in Tanzania, in the Caribbean — as part of the Black Power movement and the broader Pan-African tradition. In 1972 he published *How Europe Underdeveloped Africa*, which begins with a sentence that reframes the whole conversation: Africa was not underdeveloped. Africa was developed — into poverty. Not accidentally. Deliberately. By mechanisms that can be identified and traced. His method was to keep asking the question the shrug is designed to end: who benefits from this arrangement, and what maintains it? He was killed in Georgetown, Guyana, in 1980 — a bomb planted in a walkie-talkie — thirty-eight years old.
