The disenchanted world is not a world without violence. It is a world in which violence has been removed from legibility — in which the violence of the enclosure is called progress, the violence of the market is called efficiency, the violence of the extracted outside is called development. The enchantment that was destroyed was not an illusion of harmony. It was a form of knowing that made the actual conditions of life legible to the people living in them. That is what made response possible.
