**Elinor Ostrom** — *the commons · the eight design principles*

When she applied for PhD study, the economics department at UCLA rejected her because she hadn't taken a trigonometry course — in high school. The political science department gave her a chance, and it turned out to be the right discipline: not how markets optimize resource allocation in theory, but how humans govern shared resources in practice. In 1973 she and her husband Vincent founded the Workshop in Political Theory and Policy Analysis at Indiana — a *workshop* because they wanted knowledge built from real cases, not thought experiments. The cases accumulated for decades: irrigation in Spain and the Philippines, mountain villages in Switzerland and Japan, fisheries in Maine and Turkey, groundwater in California. From hundreds of them she distilled eight design principles — findings, not prescriptions — for what the durable commons share. In 2009 she became the first woman to win the Nobel Prize in Economics.
