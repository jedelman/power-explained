The preparation for a different world is already happening. Not in the future. Now. In communities that are taking back land. In cooperatives that are replacing profit with mutualism. In networks that are sharing care, knowledge, and resources without going through markets or states. In people saying no to enclosure and yes to commons.

This is not a plan waiting to be implemented. It is already being built.

## What's happening now

Community land trusts that have taken thousands of homes out of speculation and made them permanently affordable. Credit unions that have built an alternative to predatory banking. Cooperative farms that have restored soil and built food security. Community health networks that have trained people as healers instead of forcing them into a broken medical system. Free schools that have refused standardized curriculum and taught to the actual children in front of them. Cooperative factories that have showed how production can be organized without bosses. Indigenous territories that have maintained commons governance for generations and are now expanding it. Solidarity networks that provide care, food, and mutual aid without involving the state. Time banks. Tool libraries. Seed banks. Knowledge commons. All of these exist now.

They are small compared to the apparatus of capitalism and the state. But they exist. And they work. And they prove that it is possible.

## What the work requires

Building commons requires learning. Learning how to govern collectively. Learning practical skills. Learning history so you don't repeat it. Learning how to manage risk without privatizing it. Learning how to resolve conflict without hierarchy. All of this is teachable. All of it is already being taught.

It requires time. Commons are slower than extraction because they have to be accountable to everyone who depends on them. That is not a bug. It is a feature. It keeps the system from capturing all the wealth and heading for inevitable collapse.

It requires risk. There is no guarantee. The state will push back. Capital will push back. The infrastructure of enclosure is vast and powerful. But the only way to guarantee failure is to not try.

And it requires community. You cannot build commons alone. You cannot build them in isolation. You have to find your people. You have to show up. You have to commit to the unglamorous work of showing up over time.

> **The world you want to live in**
> **will not be built by people waiting for permission.**
> **It is built by people who start, now, where they are.**

The final piece shows how this relates to the newest enclosure: artificial intelligence and the attempt to enclose language, knowledge, and decision-making itself.
