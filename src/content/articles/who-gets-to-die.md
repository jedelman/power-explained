Achille Mbembe extended Michel Foucault's concept of biopower—the power to manage life—into territory Foucault didn't fully go. Foucault showed how modern power doesn't primarily kill. It makes life livable or not by managing health, sexuality, and population. Mbembe's question: what happens in the spaces where that management breaks down? Where the state doesn't manage life—it decides who gets to live and who is exposed to death?

He called it necropolitics. Not the power over life. The power over death. The sovereign decision about whose life counts as a life worth protecting.

## The zones where rules don't apply

In the colony. In the occupied territory. In the prison. In the poorest neighborhoods of the richest cities. There are spaces where the protections that apply elsewhere don't apply. Where violence can be applied without the justifications that legitimacy requires. Where people are exposed to death in ways the state claims not to intend but systemically produces.

Mbembe argues this is not an exception to the modern state. It's the foundation. The state's legitimacy, the law's authority—these depend on the existence of spaces where they don't apply, where power can be exercised without restraint, where people can be disposed of without the normal procedures having to be followed.

> The sovereign power doesn't just manage life.
> It **decides who is exposed to death**
> and that decision is always,
> already written into the system.

## The calculation is straightforward

Who gets protected? Who gets policed? Who gets killed without investigation? The answer maps clearly onto race, onto colonial status, onto poverty. It's not random. It's structural. It's how the system maintains itself—by making some people's lives matter and leaving others exposed.

Understanding this matters because it changes how you read the news. Every police killing that doesn't result in prosecution, every disease outbreak that moves fastest through the poorest neighborhoods, every environmental disaster sited in the territory of the least powerful—these are not failures of the system. They are the system working as designed.
