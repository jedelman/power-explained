metaphor:
  choice: |
    The walk. Not the path (which suggests a destination), not the
    journey (which suggests a return triumph), not the quest (which is
    what the walker is given, not what the walker is doing). The walk
    is what the wizard does. The walk is also what the reader has just
    finished doing, through the book. The chapter is the moment the
    walking is named as the work, and the walker is shown to be the
    next one in a line of walkers who have done the same.
  defense: |
    The metaphor is defendable because the book has been the walk all
    along — the reader has been walking through plateaus, taking doors,
    crossing terrain. To name the walk now is to name what has been
    happening, not to introduce a new figure. The defense against
    "wizard" as costume is the book's whole prior work: by the time
    the reader arrives here, the wizard has been seen plainly in the
    builders (P-04), the un-armorers (P-OA), the re-enchanters (P-RE),
    the readers-of-the-wreckage (P-AB), and the namers (P-GC). The
    walk is the verb form of all of them. No costume; the walker's
    face is the walker's face.
nagual:
  blind_spot_at_entry: |
    The reader who has walked this far is in danger of two opposite
    failures. The first is to read the wizard as an aspirational role,
    a title to be earned by reading more books, performing more rigor,
    arriving more correctly — the credential trap the whole book has
    been against. The second is to read the wizard as already-them, a
    flattering identification that lets them stop walking now that
    they have the word. The chapter has to refuse both. The walk is
    not a credential and it is not a self-image. It is a verb the
    reader either does or does not do, in their actual life, today.
  angle_of_approach: |
    Approach from praise — the reader has walked. Not flattery; the
    body has carried something. Then plainly name the wizard as the
    one who walks, and let the prior chapters' work do the heavy
    lifting (the wizard is the figure already met at the clearings,
    the floor, the herons, the Scar). Then put a specific quest in
    their hand — one thing, in one place, that only they can do —
    before the chapter is over, so the chapter cannot be set down as
    inspirational reading. Never lecture them about ethics or
    discipline; show the work being done, costs included, and trust
    them to recognize whether they want to do it.
