    The chapter seats at N — the midnight, the diagnostic, the cold clear
    view from the ridge. N is where you can see the patterns. The grimoire

    with it. The three names (production of subjectivity, hegemony, the

metaphor:
  choice: |
    The Goetia — the medieval grimoire of demons, each catalogued with a
    seal, a domain, a rank, and a specific set of instructions for summoning
    and constraining it. The Lesser Key of Solomon lists seventy-two of them.
    The chapter takes the grimoire form as its governing metaphor: capital's
    structural forces (production of subjectivity, hegemony, the outside) are
    named and sealed the way the Goetia names and seals its demons.
  defense: |
    The metaphor is defensible as commentary precisely because it is chosen
    knowing its risks. The risk is occult cosplay — aesthetic mystification
    of what is actually a materialist argument. The defense is that the
    Goetia metaphor IS the chapter's argument compressed: the grimoire's
    function is that once you have a name and a seal, you can work with
    something. You can see it coming. The naming breaks the paralysis. That
    is exactly what the chapter argues politically — conscientização, the
    reading of the word as the reading of the world. Every name in this
    chapter is a real political concept sourced from a real thinker. The
    mystical wrapping is the door; the materialist content is what you find
    inside. The Goetia is also a tradition with a prior function reassigned:
    Freire did this, Gramsci did this from prison, the chapter does it with
    the grimoire form. The reassignment is the argument.
nagual:
  blind_spot_at_entry: |
    The reader thinks the problem is individual — their own motivation,
    capability, or failure of analysis. If they are silent in the meeting,
    that is their shyness. If they cannot get a project off the ground,
    that is their skill gap. The system has been producing this interpretation
    in them since school. Their seeing runs out at the question "why am I
    like this?" — because the answer ("you were produced for a different
    kind of world") is not available inside the frame the system provided.
  angle_of_approach: |
    Approach through the writer's own failure. The restaurant case study
    (G-GC-010 through G-GC-018) shows the writer making the same mistake
    the reader is making: trying to solve a structural problem with an
    individual solution, adjusting the scaffold instead of reading the frame.
    The reader meets the argument on the writer's side of the diagnostic —
    in the exhaustion of having tried everything else first. Only after that
    exhaustion is discharged does the structural name arrive. Do not lecture
    the reader about production of subjectivity before they have felt the
    shape of it in the restaurant story. Lead with the concrete failure.
