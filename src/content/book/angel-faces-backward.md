  rationale: |
    The chapter seats at NW — the backward-facing wheel position where
    grief is processed without being looked away from. The angel literally
    faces backward into the wreckage; this is NW's posture made vivid.
    The Scar section, the chip ceremony, the kitchen floors under Hampton
    Boulevard, the failed restaurant — all live in NW. The grief is not
    the obstacle; it is the curriculum.

    It gestures toward E — community, others, the council. The chapter's
    second half opens an axial door toward E: "the angel cannot stand
    alone for long," the council passage, the distributed back-line of
    defense across temperaments and geographies. The sight the angel
    has is real, and it is too much to carry alone. The chapter's
    movement is from NW (grief seen) toward E (grief held in common)
    rather than toward S (action), because the work this chapter teaches
    is reading-and-witnessing, not building. Building is the next
    chapter (P-14, the land trust).

territory:
  feature: |
    The wizard's-cottage corner of the territory — the place where
    reading-the-wreckage is the discipline, where the practice is
    perception rather than construction. The region of Left ecological
    consciousness that knows seeing-clearly is itself a defense:
    that recognition of the auratic ground is the line already drawn,

    political stance not a private aesthetic. The territory Walter
    Benjamin walked the Paris arcades through for thirteen years. The
    territory Jason walked the Scar through. Same territory; different
    forests.

metaphor:
  choice: |
    The angel of history — Klee's painting as Benjamin read it in Thesis
    IX. Wings open and unclosable, face turned to the past, blown
    backward into the future by a storm called *progress*. The angel is
    not a symbol of despair. It is a description of the posture the
    book asks the reader to take.
  defense: |
    The metaphor is load-bearing because it is also literal. Benjamin
    bought the print in 1921. He carried it across borders. He gave it
    the meaning in 1940. He did not survive. We have the *Theses*
    because Arendt carried them. The painting is at the Israel Museum.
    Every claim about the angel is also a claim about a 32×24 cm piece
    of paper that crossed the same border Benjamin could not. The
    metaphor is the historical record refusing to be metaphor.

nagual:
  blind_spot_at_entry: |
    The reader who has just watched Graeber turn the weapon of debt
    back on the people who forged it (Ch 13, the Rolling Jubilee)
    arrives exhilarated. They have seen one example of the move done
    well. They do not yet have the practice. The blind spot is that
    they will try to skip the seeing — to move directly from "the move
    is exhilarating" to "now I will make the move" — without learning
    the posture from which the move was possible. They will look
    forward, like the system, instead of turning around. The chapter
    has to make them stop and face the wreckage first, and it has to
    do this without lecturing them out of the exhilaration.
  angle_of_approach: |
    Approach from the painting — small, faintly comic, faintly tragic.
    The image first. Then Benjamin. Then aura. Theory arrives as the
    name for what the painting already showed. The Scar lands midway,
    in Jason's first-person, so the reader's body is anchored before
    the closing argument on the auratic land. Never instruct the reader
    to grieve; the chapter sits in grief and lets them join or refuse.
    The practice at the close ("find one shard") is small, refusable,
    and offered without consequence-talk.
